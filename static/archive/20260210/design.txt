## Overall Concept and Layout

The entire single-page site is set against a deep sky-blue gradient background reminiscent of the classic Super Mario Bros. overworld sky, complete with a few parallax-scrolling white pixel-art clouds that drift slowly left to right. The ground at the very bottom of the page is a continuous strip of Mario-style brown brick and green terrain tiles that stretches the full width and scrolls subtly with a parallax effect as the user scrolls down. Scattered across the background at various depths are small decorative elements: floating pixel-art bushes, a couple of hills in the mid-ground with the characteristic Mario dotted-outline style, and occasional gold coins that rotate in place with a CSS sprite animation.

The overall vertical structure of the page is one long scrollable column. Between each major section, large green warp pipes emerge from the ground or hang from above, acting as visual connectors. As the user scrolls, a small animated Mario sprite (standing on a pipe at the top of the viewport as a fixed-position element) appears to "jump" and travel downward through the pipes. This sprite is fixed to the right margin of the screen and progresses through a simple idle/walking/jumping animation cycle tied to scroll position â€” when the user scrolls, Mario jumps; when they stop, he lands and idles. The sprite uses a pixel-art rendering style with `image-rendering: pixelated` to keep the retro crispness.

## Color Palette and Typography

The primary palette draws from the Mario universe: a rich sky blue (#5C94FC) for the background, classic pipe green (#00A800) for interactive elements and accents, brick brown (#C84C0C) for borders and structural elements, gold/yellow (#FCB838) for highlights, hover states, and coin accents, and a crisp white for text on dark backgrounds with a deep navy (#1A1A2E) for card backgrounds to keep readability. Section card backgrounds use a semi-transparent dark navy with a subtle frosted-glass blur effect.

The primary heading font is a chunky pixel-art display font like "Press Start 2P" from Google Fonts, used for the developer name, section titles, and the subtitle. Body text, project descriptions, and links use a clean modern sans-serif like Inter or Space Grotesk at comfortable reading sizes to balance the retro headings with legibility. This contrast between pixel headings and modern body text reinforces the blend of nostalgic theme with professional content.

## Interactivity and Animations

When the page first loads, a brief intro animation plays: the screen is black for half a second, then the classic Mario level-start style appears â€” a black screen with "WORLD 1-1" in pixel font and "Ã— 1" with a small developer avatar icon, which fades out after 1.5 seconds to reveal the full page with a smooth fade-in. This uses a CSS animation on an overlay div that transitions from opaque to transparent and then sets `pointer-events: none`.

All project cards enter the viewport with a subtle slide-up and fade-in animation triggered by an Intersection Observer in JavaScript, staggered by 100ms each. The pipes between sections draw themselves in (a clip-path or height animation) as the user scrolls into range.

Gold coin collectibles are scattered decoratively in the margins; when a user clicks one, it plays a brief spin-and-disappear animation with a small "+1" floating text, purely for delight. A small coin counter in the top-right corner (fixed position, styled as the Mario HUD) tracks how many coins the user has clicked, displayed in pixel font.

Hover effects on all link buttons include a slight scale-up (1.05), a brighter glow in the accent color, and a quick 50ms CSS transition. The pipe illustrations between sections have a very slow, subtle pulsing glow on their openings, as if light is emanating from inside the warp zone.

## Responsive Behavior

On mobile viewports (below 768px), the cards stretch to nearly full width with reduced padding. The decorative pipes between sections simplify to shorter vertical segments. The Mario sprite on the right margin hides to save space. The cloud parallax effect reduces intensity. The hero block row stacks more compactly, and the social link icons in the final card stack vertically rather than horizontally. The intro animation still plays but shortens to 1 second total. All pixel-art elements maintain their `image-rendering: pixelated` quality at any scale.
