The website features a deep indigo, high-resolution selvedge denim background with a subtle, tactile twill texture. The core interactive mechanism revolves around the cursor acting as an "unzipper" or "seam-ripper". As the user scrolls or moves the mouse, subtle tears and unzipped seams dynamically open in the denim fabric, revealing a dark, glowing underlayer of colorful HTML, JavaScript, and CSS code representing the scaffolding of the website. The primary color palette utilizes navy blue for the canvas, golden-orange to simulate thick Levi’s-style stitching for typography and borders, and polished copper for buttons and interactive points. 

At the top of the single-page layout lies the Hero Section, designed as a large, distressed leather brand patch stitched directly onto the digital denim canvas. The developer's name, "Geva Kipper", is boldly debossed into the leather using a clean, modern serif font. Below it, faded onto the leather like a vintage fabric stamp, reads a single sentence: "Currently engineering innovative software solutions at Google." Hanging from the bottom right corner of this leather patch is a highly realistic woven red tag—similar to a clothing care label—that flutters slightly on hover, displaying the subtitle: "Re-designed daily by AI. An ever-changing digital canvas."

The main body of the site consists of a masonry-style grid of eight interactive sections, framed as different denim features like pockets, fabric swatches, and stitched-on patches. 
Box 1 (GlglStats) is styled as a back pocket with golden thread tracing a soundwave pattern. On hover, the pocket pulls down slightly to reveal a data-viz graphic of LZ77 compression metrics. Copper rivet buttons serve as links to the live project (glglstats.geekip.dev) and a play-button icon links to the PyData TLV presentation on YouTube.
Box 2 (Ahuzat Dibuk) takes the form of a faded, sun-bleached heat-map printed directly onto the fabric, representing the Tel-Aviv parking data. Three embroidered merit badges are ironed onto this section, serving as interactive, tactile links to the news coverage on Channel 13, Mako, and the original Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post. 

Box 3 (2D Part Orienting) is a rigid, geometric dark-wash denim patch held down by heavy cross-stitching. Polygonal shapes are dynamically "pushed" into alignment via animated dashed lines inside the box. A heavy brass snap button serves as the link to the CGL project page and C++ code repository. 
Box 4 (The Turing Jest) is a dual-colored fabric swatch split down the middle with a jagged zig-zag stitch, representing the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete at puns. Comedy and tragedy masks are embroidered in silver thread, with a hanging cardboard price tag providing the link to jest.geekip.dev.

Box 5 (Rhymes of Israel) utilizes a high school yearbook aesthetic, presenting as a glossy Polaroid photo pinned to the denim with a shiny silver safety pin. The photo displays a comedic LLM-generated rhymed headline, and the photo’s classic white lower border features a "Download" sticker that links out to the Chrome Web Store installation page.
Box 6 (Hatul Basak) features tiny golden paw prints stitched across the fabric, leading to a Wikipedia globe made of jigsaw puzzle pieces. A woven loop tag, seamlessly integrated into the top seam of the box, provides the link to hatulbasak.geekip.dev where users can play AI daily challenges and create their own catfishing.net style puzzles.

Box 7 (The Meta Portfolio) breaks the structural rules of the page. It is a highly realistic ripped hole in the jeans, with frayed white threads hanging over the edge. Peeking vividly through the rip is a neon-glowing, matrix-like terminal interface detailing the website’s own architecture. The glow illuminates the literal self-reference link, geekip.dev, hovering in the dark void behind the denim. 
Box 8 (Socials) sits at the bottom of the grid, composed of three distressed denim belt loops holding up a sleek digital utility belt. Embossed onto shiny metal belt buckles are the modern icons and clickable links for GitHub (github.com/gevak), LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and Facebook (facebook.com/geva.kipper).

The Footer is styled as the folded-up bottom hem of the jeans, revealing the iconic white and red-striped selvedge edge underneath. Stamped across this inner fabric in bold, stenciled white letters is the credit: "Woven exclusively by Artificial Intelligence." Tucked into a tiny, overlapping coin pocket on the right side of the hem is a glowing green LCD digital stopwatch component. Powered by JavaScript, it continuously ticks upward, aggressively tracking and displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds that have elapsed since the chronological anchor point of 2026-05-20 02:39:57.
