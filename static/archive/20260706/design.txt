The core of the website is an interactive, hardware-accelerated Zoomable User Interface (ZUI) built over a massive WebGL canvas. Rather than scrolling vertically, the user uses their mouse wheel or trackpad to zoom seamlessly in and out of a deeply complex, slowly animating mathematical fractal (such as a Mandelbulb or a bespoke Julia set). The color palette is composed of high-contrast obsidian and luminescent neon accents, which the logic implies changes dynamically every 24 hours. Standard UI elements—text, buttons, and media—are rendered as frosted "glassmorphism" HTML overlays that become visible, scaling up and locking into view into readable content boxes as the user zooms into specific coordinates of the fractal. 

Fixed at the top-left of the viewport, untouched by the zooming canvas, is a minimalist HUD (Heads Up Display). It reads: "Geva Kipper" in a bold, futuristic sans-serif typeface, followed by the sentence: "Software Engineer at Google architecting massively scalable backend systems and infrastructure." directly beneath it, a subtle, glitch-effect subtitle reads: "Note: The geometry and layout of this portfolio are autonomously re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing. You are viewing today's unique iteration."

The hidden resume easter eggs are integrated directly into the deepest layers of the fractal design. As a user zooms past the main project boxes into the negative space of the spiraling graphics, micro-typography crystallizes into view. For instance, zooming 500% into a specific geometric swirl reveals text like "Experience: Software Engineer at Google (202x - Present)", or "Core Stack: Python, C++, Node.js" embedded along the curvature of the fractal lines.

The main content is distributed across eight spatial nodes (glassmorphism cards) anchored within the fractal, discovered as the user zooms into different "galaxies" of the canvas:

**Node 1: The Data Spiral.** Zooming into the top-right quadrant reveals the "GlglStats" project. The UI box features a visual abstraction of a soundwave and explains the Python project measuring the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs using LZ77 compression and visualizing repetition patterns. It features two prominent buttons: one linking to the live app at https://glglstats.geekip.dev, and an embedded YouTube iframe playing the PyData TLV presentation (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo).

**Node 2: The Urban Grid.** Nestled in a dense, grid-like section of the fractal is "Ahuzat Dibuk". The layout explains the Python scraping and data visualization platform collecting Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy statistics to generate heat-maps for residents. It contains a row of sleek badge-links directing out to the news coverage on Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/), Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and the original viral Facebook post in 'Secret Tel Aviv' (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).

**Node 3: The Algorithmic Vertex.** Zooming into a sharp, angular geometric branch reveals "2D Part Orienting". This box highlights the C++ open-source computational geometry implementation of an efficient algorithm constructing a 'push plan' to orient polygons. A glowing terminal-style link connects to the research lab's page: https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/.

**Node 4 & 5: The AI Nebula.** Located in a highly turbulent, colorful area of the canvas are two adjacent nodes. Node 4 is "The Turing Jest", an LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete at generating and explaining puns, rated by users, with a button linking to https://jest.geekip.dev. Beside it, Node 5 is "Rhymes of Israel," featuring a playful, magazine-style layout within the frosted glass card, describing the Chrome extension that uses LLMs to rewrite Israeli news headlines into comedic high-school yearbook rhymes, linking directly to the Chrome Web Store: https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb.

**Node 6: The Trivia Nexus.** Deep within an interconnected web of fractal points sits "Hatul Basak". The description explains this Hebrew Wikipedia-based trivia game (a port of catfishing.net), detailing its AI-generated daily challenges and user-created themed puzzles. A bold call-to-action button links to https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev to play and create puzzles.

**Node 7: The Droste Effect.** Zooming into the exact center of the entire fractal reveals a recursive, meta box: a miniature, fully stylized version of the exact portfolio website the user is currently looking at. It contains a self-referential link pointing back to https://geekip.dev, acknowledging the portfolio project itself.

**Node 8: The Outer Ring.** Placed at the extreme zoomed-out edges of the global canvas is a sleek, horizontal control panel displaying interactive icons for social connections, ensuring they are easily accessible from a bird's-eye view. The links map to github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper.

Pinned to the absolute bottom margin of the screen, floating above the fractal, is a sleek digital footer. It reads: "Architected by an AI Language Model." Next to this, a monospaced digital stopwatch constantly ticks upwards in real-time, displaying exactly how many hours, minutes, and seconds have elapsed since the temporal anchor point of `2026-07-06 02:33:00`.
