The website features an immersive, top-down 2.5D view of a vibrant, sunlit summer coastline. The left side of the screen is composed of soft, golden sand, which transitions smoothly into a vast expanse of crystal-clear turquoise ocean on the right. The water is animated using modern CSS and WebGL shaders to simulate gentle, rhythmic, foamy waves lapping against the shore. The entire single-page layout acts as a seamless canvas without traditional scrolling limits; instead, users can pan up and down the sprawling shoreline. 

In the top left "hero" section, a weathered wooden driftwood signpost is staked into the sand. Carved elegantly into the wood is the name "Geva Kipper", with a subtitle painted directly below in faded white nautical lettering: "Software engineer currently solving complex problems at Google." Right beside the post, traced deeply into the wet sand—animated via SVG stroke drawing as the site loads—is the text: "Note: This landscape shifts like the tides. This website is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." 

Instead of traditional grid boxes, the eight core sections are represented by beautifully rendered, translucent glass bottles with rustic corks. Some are half-buried in the wet sand, while others gently bob dynamically in the shallow ocean waves. Hovering over a bottle creates a subtle ripple effect in the water and causes the bottle to tilt. Clicking a bottle creates a cinematic transition: a soft "pop" animation removes the cork, and a rolled-up piece of parchment elegantly unfurls into the center of the screen. The background beach gracefully blurs out behind a frosted glass overlay (backdrop-filter) while the parchment modal displays the project details.

The contents of the eight unfurled parchment scrolls are designed as follows:
**Bottle 1: GlglStats** – The parchment has slightly charred, aged edges. The header utilizes an animated, looping visualization of LZ77 compression applied to musical notes. The text describes the Python project measuring the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs. Included is a prominent "View Live Site" wax seal button linking to `https://glglstats.geekip.dev`, alongside an embedded, stylized frame playing the YouTube presentation from the PyData TLV conference (`https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`). 

**Bottle 2: Ahuzat Dibuk** – The background of this scroll features a faint, blue-ink watercolour heatmap of Tel-Aviv. The typography details the Python scraping and data visualization project for Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancies. Below the text, three elegant anchor icons link to the press coverage on Channel 13 (`https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/`), Mako (`https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm`), and the original viral Facebook post (`https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/`).

**Bottle 3: 2D Part Orienting** – The parchment is overlaid with a subtle, geometric grid. Clean, architectural wireframe diagrams of polygons animate softly as they unfold. The text explains the C++ open-source computational geometry implementation used to generate push plans. A technical blueprint-style button links to the source and documentation at `https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/`.

**Bottle 4: The Turing Jest** – Designed with a medieval, theatrical motif. Faded comedy and tragedy masks watermark the background. The text describes the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete in making and explaining puns based on user ratings. A colorful, jester-hat icon serves as a pulsing hyperlink to `https://jest.geekip.dev`.

**Bottle 5: Rhymes of Israel** – This scroll takes a more modern, eclectic twist, styled like a torn, doodle-filled page from a high school yearbook seamlessly taped to the ancient parchment. The text outlines the Chrome extension that uses an LLM to rewrite Israeli news headlines into comedic rhymes. A hand-sketched Chrome Web Store logo links directly to the extension installation page over at the Chrome Web Store (`https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb`).

**Bottle 6: Hatul Basak** – Faint, ink-stained cat paw prints track diagonally across this scroll. The text introduces the Hebrew port of catfishing.net, a Wikipedia-based Trivia game powered by AI-generated daily challenges and user-created puzzles. A sleek, golden trivia-coin button directs users to play or craft puzzles at `https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev`.

**Bottle 7: The Meta Portfolio** – The bottle for this section is uniquely distinct, refracting light in a kaleidoscopic, fractal pattern. The scroll inside features a hand-drawn, recursive illustration of a beach containing a bottle containing a beach. The text highlights the meta nature of this very portfolio site. A stylized self-referential loop icon links to `https://geekip.dev`.

**Bottle 8: Social Connections** – Resting atop a sunlit, algae-covered rock in the water, this final bottle looks like an ornate pirate rum flask. The unfurled parchment reveals a classic treasure map aesthetic. At the center is a compass rose where "X marks the spot" for his digital footprint, providing distinct, stylized navigational points linking to `github.com/gevak`, `linkedin.com/in/gevakip`, and `facebook.com/geva.kipper`.

At the very bottom of the expansive shoreline, resting beside a cluster of seashells, sits an antique, half-buried golden pocket watch and a smooth, glowing piece of sea glass. The sea glass is etched with a luminescent inscription that reads: "Design conceptualized by an AI, manifesting waves of code." The open face of the pocket watch features a glowing digital mechanism that acts as a live, constantly ticking stopwatch displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds that flip seamlessly since the timestamp '2026-07-21 02:00:36'.
