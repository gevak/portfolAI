**Overall Concept & Visual Architecture**
The website is structured as a vertical scroll that creates an illusion of forward movement through a dimly lit, modern 3D museum corridor. The overall color palette is a deep, atmospheric dark void (e.g., `#050508`) with subtle, frosted floor reflections. Interactions are driven by CSS 3D transforms (`perspective`, `rotateY`, `rotateX`, `translateZ`) tied to the userâ€™s scroll position. As the user scrolls down, they "walk" past glowing digital pedestals positioned along the left and right sides of the screen. Above each pedestal floats a glassmorphic "hologram" card containing the project details. Typography should be a sleek, geometric sans-serif (like 'Space Grotesk') paired with a technical monospace font for subtitles and links.

**The Foyer (Hero Section)**
The immediate landing view acts as the museum entrance. Centered on the screen is a large, elegant title reading "Geva Kipper", illuminated by a soft, pulsating back-glow (box-shadow). Below it, in crisp, minimalist text: "Currently building and engineering solutions at Google." 
Directly beneath this is the AI subtitle. To emphasize the ever-changing nature of the text, use an ethereal, slightly glitchy or fading CSS animation that reads: "This digital gallery is re-designed each day by AI and is endlessly ever-changing." Wait a moment before the user is prompted to "Scroll to Enter," initiating the forward-movement parallax effect into the gallery.

**The Hologram Pedestals (Projects 1-6)**
Each project is presented as a holographic display floating above a minimalist metallic base. The "holograms" are translucent, frosted-glass CSS panels with semi-transparent borders and individual neon drop-shadow glows. By default, they are angled in 3D space (leaning slightly toward the center walkway). When the user hovers over a display, it smoothly rotates to face the user flat-on, and the glow intensifies.

*   **Exhibit 1: GlglStats**
    *   *Visuals:* A cyan-glowing hologram. The background of the card features a faint, looping CSS animation of expanding and collapsing soundwaves or barcode-like strips, visually representingLZ77 compression and pop-music repetition.
    *   *Content:* Title, brief description of the Python project and song visualization.
    *   *Interactive:* Two glowing, pill-shaped buttons: one directing to the live site (`https://glglstats.geekip.dev`), and another featuring a minimalist play-icon linking to the PyData TLV YouTube presentation (`https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`).
*   **Exhibit 2: Ahuzat Dibuk**
    *   *Visuals:* A crimson and orange heatmap-colored hologram. The background uses an animated CSS gradient mesh shifting through red, orange, and yellow to mimic a dynamic parking occupancy heatmap.
    *   *Content:* Title, description of the Python scraping and Tel-Aviv parking data.
    *   *Interactive:* A staggered list of glowing textual links to the media coverage (Channel 13, Mako) and the original Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook publication.
*   **Exhibit 3: 2D Part Orienting**
    *   *Visuals:* An electric blue, highly technical hologram. Inside the glass card, a geometric, neon polygon continuously rotates while being "pushed" into alignment by invisible glowing grid lines.
