**Overall Aesthetic and Concept**
The layout operates as a cinematic, interactive space-flight experience, blending elegant glassmorphism with retro-arcade mechanics. The single-page architecture utilizes a deep obsidian `#0b0c10` parallax background filled with drifting starfields and subtle neon nebulas. Instead of a traditional cursor, the user commands a sleek, glowing wireframe spacecraft anchored to the bottom-center of the viewport. Scrolling down accelerates the ship forward "through" space, while lateral mouse movements subtly bank the ship left and right. The website's content sections exist as stylized, rotating 3D asteroids floating in the void. As the user scrolls, these asteroids approach. Hovering the cursor over an asteroid "targets" it, snapping a delicate cyan crosshair onto the object. This lock-on triggers a sleek, frosted-glass HUD panel to expand alongside the asteroid, revealing the project details. Interacting with the buttons fires a neon laser from the spacecraft to the target, handling the hyperlink transition.

**Hero Section (The Launch Pad)**
At the pinnacle of the page, before the scrolling voyage begins, a minimalist, high-contrast typographical lockup dominates the screen. In a bold, futuristic sans-serif font (like Orbitron), the text reads: "Geva Kipper." Immediately below, an elegant, monospace sub-heading serves as the HUD's mission briefing: "Software Engineer contributing to the future of technology at Google." Below this, highlighted in a shifting, iridescent text gradient, is the thematic subtitle: "System Reality: Re-designed daily by AI. The universe is ever-changing." A pulsing arrow prompts the user to "Scroll to Initiate Thrusters," fading out as the ship begins its ascent.

**Asteroid Projects (The Scrolling Targets)**
As the user scrolls, eight distinct celestial objects approach, each representing a project or section. When targeted, their respective glassmorphic data panels expand, utilizing elegant typography (Inter or Roboto) to display the required links as sleek, glowing HUD buttons.

**Target 1: GlglStats** – Designed as a crystalline asteroid pulsing in rhythm with an invisible beat. The expanded HUD describes the Python project measuring repetitiveness in Israeli pop-chart songs via LZ77 compression, complete with data visualizations. The panel features two laser-targetable buttons: "[Launch Project](https://glglstats.geekip.dev)" and "[View PyData TLV Presentation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo)".

**Target 2: Ahuzat Dibuk** – Visualized as an asteroid wrapped in a glowing, multi-colored thermal heat-map grid. The text details the Python scraping and data visualization tool tracking Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy. Targetable links include: "[Channel 13 Feature](https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/)", "[Mako Tech Feature](https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm)", and "[Original Secret Tel Aviv Post](https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/)".

**Target 3: 2D Part Orienting** – Rendered as a complex, rotating geometric polygon asteroid that constantly recalibrates its axis. The panel describes the C++ implementation inside an open-source computational geometry library, detailing an efficient push-plan algorithm to orient polygons. The single targetable holographic button reads: "[Access Code & Documentation](https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/)".

**Target 4: The Turing Jest** – Designed as twin asteroids orbiting each other, subtly resembling the classic comedy and tragedy masks built out of glowing circuitry. The panel introduces the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs battle through puns and are ranked by users. The hyper-drive link reads: "[Enter The Jest Arena](https://jest.geekip.dev)".

**Target 5: Rhymes of Israel** – A metallic asteroid emitting a trail of digital stardust shaped like Hebrew characters. The text outlines the Chrome extension that utilizes LLMs to rewrite Israeli news headlines into comedic, high school yearbook-style rhymes. The installation target reads: "[Install Chrome Extension](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb)".

**Target 6: Hatul Basak** – Shaped like a sleek, digitized feline silhouette drifting through the cosmos. The HUD details a Hebrew port of catfishing.net, providing Wikipedia-based Trivia challenges utilizing AI-generated daily puzzles and user-created themes. The interface target reads: "[Play & Create Puzzles](https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev)".

**Target 7: The System Itself (Meta Portfolio)** – A perfectly smooth, mirror-like sphere that reflects the spaceship and the surrounding starlight—a meta-reference to the portfolio itself. The HUD text playfully breaks the fourth wall to acknowledge the site you are currently navigating. The self-referencing laser link reads: "[Recalibrate System](https://geekip.dev)".

**Target 8: Communications Array (Socials)** – The final target before the scroll exhausts is a floating space station with three slowly spinning orbital rings. Hovering over it locks onto the developer’s specific transmission frequencies. Three tactical UI targets appear: "[GitHub Database](github.com/gevak)", "[LinkedIn Network](linkedin.com/in/gevakip)", and "[Facebook Comm-Link](facebook.com/geva.kipper)".

**Footer (The Navigational Dashboard)**
At the absolute bottom of the scroll, the viewport settles into a fixed "Cockpit Dashboard" view. The elegant structural UI features scanning lines and terminal text. The text credits the creation of the current layout to "Navigational AI Architect." Beside the text, a glaring red digital stopwatch continuously counts up (Hours : Minutes : Seconds), preceded by the label: "Time elapsed since system epoch [2026-06-23 02:37:57]". As seconds tick by in real-time, tiny thruster particles intermittently fire in the background to keep the site feeling alive and ever-changing.
