The overall aesthetic of the single-page website is modeled after a high-end, audiophile turntable setup, utilizing a dark, elegant color palette of brushed gunmetal gray, matte black, and warm brass accents. The page utilizes a scroll-jacking mechanism where scrolling does not move the page up and down, but rather drives interactive, cinematic 3D/2D layered CSS animations of the record player changing "tracks." 

At the top of the page, acting as the "Amplifier" unit of the record player, a sleek, glowing VFD (Vacuum Fluorescent Display) panel presents the header. In crisp, warm-amber typography, it displays the developer's name: "Geva Kipper." Immediately below it is a single sentence: "Software Engineer at Google, focusing on building scalable and innovative software solutions." Beneath this, a subtle, scrolling marquee-style subtitle reads: "Notice: This digital interface is creatively re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." 

The centerpiece of the screen is a top-down, slightly isometric view of the interactive vinyl record player. The record itself is divided into eight distinct, visible grooves (representing the eight sections). Next to the turntable is a dynamic stack of vintage-style cardboard vinyl sleeves. As the user scrolls down, the turntable's brass tonearm physically lifts and drops onto the corresponding groove, the vinyl begins to spin, and the relevant "album sleeve" slides gracefully out from the stack to the center-right of the screen, acting as a project container.

Track 1 is an album sleeve titled "GlglStats." Its cover art features a colorful, heavily compressed soundwave visualization. The "liner notes" on the sleeve describe the Python project, which measures the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs using LZ77 compression and visualizes repetition patterns in famous songs. Interactive buttons stylize the links: a "Launch Project" button points to https://glglstats.geekip.dev, and a "Watch Live at PyData TLV" button points to https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo.

Scrolling to Track 2 triggers the tonearm to move inward. The sleeve titled "Ahuzat Dibuk" slides out, featuring a glowing heatmap of Tel Aviv as its artwork. The text describes a Python scraping and data visualization project that collects statistics on Tel Aviv car parking lots occupancy to generate heatmaps for residents. A "Press & Media" section provides elegant hyperlink buttons to the news stories at https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/ and https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm, alongside an "Original Facebook Release" link pointing to https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/.

Track 3 slides into view as "2D Part Orienting," decorated with sharp, abstract, intersecting polygons. The text highlights a C++ implementation in an open-source computational geometry library of an efficient algorithm constructing a "push plan" to orient a given polygon. A geometric button links to additional info and code at https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/.

Track 4 reveals "The Turing Jest," featuring cover art of two AI robots wearing jester hats facing off in a comedic duel. The notes explain it is an LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete at making and explaining puns, rated by users. A prominent neon "Enter the Arena" button links to https://jest.geekip.dev.

Track 5, "Rhymes of Israel," utilizes a retro, high-school yearbook aesthetic for its album art. The sleeve details a Chrome extension that uses an LLM to rewrite Israeli news site headlines into comedic rhymes. An "Add to Chrome" button links directly to https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb.

Track 6 presents "Hatul Basak," styled as a Wikipedia page peeling back to reveal a trivia board over a playful cat silhouette. The description explains it is a Hebrew port of catfishing.net, a Wikipedia-based trivia game using AI to generate daily challenges, paired with user-created themed puzzles. A "Play & Create Puzzles" button links to https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev.

Track 7 acts as the "Meta Track," titled "Portfolio Project." The album art humorously displays an infinite, recursive mirror effect of the very turntable the user is interacting with. The text acknowledges the self-referential nature of building this exact site, accompanied by a "Loop Back" link navigating to https://geekip.dev.

Track 8 represents the "B-Side / Artist Info," serving as the networking section. The sleeve is minimalistic, featuring only gold foil-stamped logos. Three distinct buttons are presented for connecting with the developer: GitHub (github.com/gevak), LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and Facebook (facebook.com/geva.kipper).

At the bottom of the viewport, styled as an engraved, brushed-metal manufacturer's plate on the base of the turntable, exists the footer. Engraved text reads: "Design & Mechanics engineered by [AI Model Name]." Embedded directly into this metal plate is a glowing red liquid-crystal stopwatch, actively ticking in a live "HH:MM:SS" format, calculating and displaying the precise amount of time continually elapsed since the specific timestamp of 2026-04-12 01:58:07.
