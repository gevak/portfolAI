The Chamber of Echoes

GlglStats is a tool for visualizing the activity of Israeli army radio stations ("Galgalatz" and "Galei Tzahal"). It scrapes real-time broadcast data and presents it as interactive statistics and dashboards. I presented this project at the "August Penguin" conference.

The Overlook of Ahuzat Dibuk

"Ahuzat Dibuk" is a data project that created a parking availability heat-map for Tel Aviv. By analyzing parking payment data for 250,000 parking spots, it provided drivers with a predictive model of where they are most likely to find a spot, which was featured in major Israeli news outlets.

The Forge of Geometry

As part of my M.Sc. at Tel-Aviv University, I co-developed a C++ algorithm for 2D Part Orienting. The goal is to find the optimal orientation for a polygonal part to minimize the support structure needed for 3D printing, a crucial step in efficient additive manufacturing. The code and paper are available through the university.

The Jester's Arena

The Turing Jest was a competition where participants submitted bots (LLMs) to engage in humorous conversations. We developed a platform with an Elo rating system to pit the bots against each other. The goal was to create the funniest chatbot, judged by human voters in head-to-head "roast battles".

The Scribe's Library

"Rhymes of Israel" is a whimsical Chrome extension that automatically finds and replaces headlines on major Israeli news sites with rhyming, often humorous, alternatives. It's a small project to bring a bit of levity to the news-reading experience.
