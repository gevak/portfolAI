The website visually adopts a sleek, single-page, retro-futuristic aesthetic centering around a large, highly detailed vector graphic of an illuminated Japanese-style 80s vending machine. The overall color palette utilizes deep atmospheric hues like charcoal and navy, accented by vibrant neon pinks, cyans, and ambers to create an elegant but playful "cyberpunk meets modern minimalism" vibe. The entire UI is contained within the viewport, utilizing glassmorphism (frosted glass) effects for the machine's main window and displaying the projects as glowing, stylized physical items on the internal shelves. 

The interactive mechanic relies on a "Digital Wallet" fixed to the right side of the screen, containing a stack of 8-bit virtual gold coins. The user must drag and drop a coin into the vending machine’s blinking coin slot. Upon insertion, a satisfying CSS animation turns on the machine's mechanical keypad. The user can then click the keypad to select an item. The selected item drops into the dispenser tray at the bottom, which immediately triggers a smooth JavaScript transition, expanding the item into an elegant, frosted-glass modal overlay containing the project's details and links.

The header is integrated directly into the top of the vending machine. The marquee signage features a glowing neon typography displaying the developer's name: **Geva Kipper**. Below the name, an animated LED dot-matrix ticker reads: "Software Engineer at Google." Below the ticker, a sleek metallic plaque displays the subtitle: "Warning: Interface re-designed daily by AI. Ever-changing layout." 

Inside the machine's glass display pane, the seven requested sections are designed as retro tech products across three shelves. 
**Slot A1 (GlglStats)** is styled as a glitching audio cassette. Expanding it reveals the Python/LZ77 Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness analysis, with glowing buttons linking to the live site (https://glglstats.geekip.dev) and the PyData TLV YouTube video (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo).
**Slot A2 (Ahuzat Dibuk)** is represented by a miniature, glowing heat-map display. The expanded modal explains the Tel-Aviv parking lot statistics project, featuring a row of "press passes" that link to the news stories (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/ and https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm) and the Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook post (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).
**Slot A3 (2D Part Orienting)** takes the form of a hovering crystalline polygon. The modal breaks down the C++ computational geometry push-plan algorithm, housing a sleek button linked to the library project (https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/).

On the second shelf, **Slot B1 (The Turing Jest)** is designed as a holographic comedy/tragedy mask. The text highlights the LMSYS-like LLM pun arena, with a primary call-to-action button linking to https://jest.geekip.dev.
**Slot B2 (Rhymes of Israel)** is styled as a physical high-school yearbook embossed with the Google Chrome logo. The modal details the comedic LLM news headline extension, offering an "Install" button routed to the Chrome Web Store (https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb).
**Slot B3 (Portfolio Website)** is playfully designed as a miniature, glowing replica of the vending machine itself. Its modal emphasizes the meta-nature of the project, linking internally to https://geekip.dev.

The bottom shelf features the final section, **Slot C1 (Social Network Fuel)**, designed as a sleek 3-pack of energy drink cans bearing the logos of GitHub, LinkedIn, and Facebook. Dispensing this item opens a streamlined modal with three large, interactive icon buttons linking out accordingly (github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, facebook.com/geva.kipper).

The footer is integrated into the heavy brushed-metal base plate of the vending machine. Engraved directly into the metal texture is a credit to the AI designer: "System Architecture and Visual Design generated by an Artificial Intelligence." To the right of the chassis, an inset red LED digital display serves as an uptime tracker. Using a JavaScript interval, it functions as a live stopwatch, ticking up the precise hours, minutes, and seconds that have continuously passed since a fixed temporal anomaly: 2026-04-07 01:51:10.
