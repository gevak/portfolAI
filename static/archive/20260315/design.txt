**Global Layout and Art Direction**
The website is a single-page parallax scrolling experience styled as an interactive, ancient cartographic map (a Roman "Tabula"). The background texture mimics aged vellum or parchment (warm ivory and faded sepia). Topographic lines and coastlines define the landscape. The theme merges antiquity with modern software engineering: dead digital "bugs" are drawn as defeated mythological monsters on the fringes of the map, and data pipelines are illustrated as towering Roman aqueducts. The color palette pairs elegant, historic tones—terracotta, imperial gold, and deep iron grey—with striking bursts of modern "terminal cyan" and "syntax-highlight pink" that illuminate on hover. As the user scrolls down, they traverse a cobblestone road (the Via Appia) connecting various "provinces" (the portfolio sections). The cursor is styled as a minimalist Roman gladius sword.

**The Capital (Header Section)**
The journey begins at the top center of the map in "Rome." The centerpiece is a grand, minimalist vector-art temple. Floating above it in an elegant, Trajan-inspired sans-serif font is the developer's name: "Geva Kipper." Below the name, engraved in a marble-textured banner, is a single sentence: "Currently engineering the legions of the modern world as a Software Engineer at Google." Floating just beneath this banner is an unfurled digital scroll containing the subtitle, written in an elegant tech-monospaced font: "This realm is re-designed anew each day by artificial intelligence; an ever-changing empire."

**The Provinces (Projects 1 to 3)**
Following the cobblestone road downward, the user encounters the first three "conquered territories," each housed in a distinct Roman-themed map motif. When hovered, the sepia-toned structures ignite with neon digital grids and interactive UI elements. 
*   **Province I (GlglStats)** is depicted as the "Amphitheater of Repetition." A glowing LZ77 compression algorithm is etched into the arena sand. The text describes the Python project measuring Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness. Two interactive golden icons are here: a lyre leading to https://glglstats.geekip.dev, and an amphitheater mask linking to the PyData TLV YouTube video.
*   **Province II (Ahuzat Dibuk)** is styled as an ancient "Chariot Garrison." An overlaid, glowing heat-map radiates from the stables, visualizing the Tel-Aviv parking data scraping project. Interactive parchment scrolls provide links to the news stories (13tv, Mako) and the original Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook post.
*   **Province III (2D Part Orienting)** takes the form of an "Architect’s Guild." The graphic features a drafting table with polygons, compasses, and C++ code interwoven with algorithmic push plans. A pulsing blueprint icon links out to the computational geometry library page.

**The Provinces (Projects 4 to 6)**
Continuing along the digital aqueduct, the final three project hubs appear.
*   **Province IV (The Turing Jest)** is an imposing "Colosseum of Wits." Inside, holographic gladiator silhouettes (representing LLMs) battle using glowing speech bubbles. The description explains the LMSYS-like pun-arena, and crossed gladius swords link to https://jest.geekip.dev.
*   **Province V (Rhymes of Israel)** appears as the "Oracle’s Scribe." The visual is a high-school yearbook reimagined as a Roman fresco, where comedic rhymes emerge from an ancient stylus. A brass-forged Chrome logo serves as the download link for the extension.
*   **Province VI (The Pantheon of Mirrors)** serves as the meta portfolio project. It is drawn as a domed Pantheon containing an infinite, glowing terminal mirror reflecting the map itself. A compass rose in the center provides the self-referential link to https://geekip.dev.

**The Harbor and The Milestone (Socials and Footer)**
At the bottom of the map, the road terminates at a bustling coastal port—the edge of the empire. 
*   **Section 7 (Social Network Fleet):** Three Roman galleys are docked in the glowing cyan water. Each ship's sail bears a crest: an Octocat wrapped in a laurel wreath (github.com/gevak), an 'in' shield (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and the Facebook insignia (facebook.com/geva.kipper). Clicking a ship triggers a "setting sail" CSS animation before navigating to the profile.
*   **The Footer:** On the shore stands a central Roman milestone (Milliarium Aureum). Carved into its stone base is the AI credit: "Cartography and architecture forged by [AI Model Name]." Embedded into the center of the ancient stone is a starkly modern digital sundial: an active, ticking JavaScript stopwatch displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds that have elapsed since "2026-03-15 01:50:32".
