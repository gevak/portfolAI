**Global Aesthetic & Layout Strategy**
The website operates on a pure dark mode canvas (deep space black or #0B0B0E) to maximize the contrast of light elements. The core visual engine is an interactive, overarching "Dark Side of the Moon" style prism theme powered by modern CSS, HTML, and JavaScript. As the user scrolls vertically down the single page, a continuous animated white beam of light tracks their progress. At a central point in the hero section, this beam collides with a 3D-styled, glassmorphic prism, refracting instantly into a seven-color ROYGBIV spectrum array. These seven distinct, glowing, animated neon lines snake down the rest of the page, acting as visual tethers connecting to the seven respective content boxes. 

**Hero Section & Typography**
The topmost section features a minimalist, centered layout. A stark white, glowing beam of light enters from the top left corner. Floating elegantly above the interactive 3D prism is the developer’s name: "Geva Kipper" in a bold, sleek sans-serif font (like Inter or clash Display) in pure white. Directly underneath, rendered in a slightly muted silver, is the sentence: "Software Engineer at Google, shaping the future of tech." 
Below this, leveraging a subtle glitch or dynamic typing animation in JavaScript to emphasize its algorithmic nature, is the subtitle: "This website is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." 

**The Refraction & The Project Arrays (The 7 Boxes)**
Upon scrolling past the hero prism, the single white beam officially splits. The layout transitions away from a standard grid into an alternating, cascading timeline. Each of the seven boxes is tethered to one of the specific colored light beams (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet). 
The boxes heavily utilize a Glassmorphism aesthetic: they are semi-transparent, deep frost-blurred panes (`backdrop-filter: blur(16px)`), outlined by a 1px border of their designated spectrum color, and project a soft, colored neon drop-shadow. When a user hovers over a box, an interactive JavaScript event causes that specific colored light beam to pulse and the box’s inner glow to intensify.

*   **Box 1 (The Red Ray - GlglStats):** A deep ruby glow. The text introduces 'GlglStats', a Python project measuring the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs utilizing LZ77 compression, complete with visual repetition patterns of famous tracks. The UI features two prominent, glowing red pill-shaped buttons: one directing to `https://glglstats.geekip.dev` and another housing an embedded thumbnail or lightbox link to the PyData TLV presentation at `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`.
*   **Box 2 (The Orange Ray - Ahuzat Dibuk):** A vibrant amber glow. The content describes the Python scraping and data visualization project for Tel-Aviv car parking occupancy and its generated heat-maps. Under the description, rowed out like metallic badges, are links to the press coverage: Channel 13 (`https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/`), Mako (`https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm`), and the original viral Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post (`https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/`).
*   **Box 3 (The Yellow Ray - 2D Part Orienting):** A bright, electric yellow. Dedicated to the C++ computational geometry library implementation for constructing a 'push plan' to orient a given polygon. The layout here should feel highly technical and structural, featuring a minimal chevron-link to the code and additional info at `https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/`.
*   **Box 4 (The Green Ray - The Turing Jest):** A neon emerald glow. The description covers the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete in making and explaining puns, rated by users. A fun, slightly playful typography can be used for the title here, alongside a glowing green portal-style link to `https://jest.geekip.dev`.
*   **Box 5 (The Blue Ray - Rhymes of Israel):** A cool cyan/sapphire glow. Highlights the Chrome extension utilizing an LLM to rewrite Israeli news headlines into comedic, high-school yearbook-style rhymes. A sleek "Add to Chrome" styled custom button anchors this card, linking to the Chrome Web Store: `https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb`.
*   **Box 6 (The Indigo Ray - The Meta Portfolio):** A deep, electric indigo glow. This box is highly self-referential ("how meta!"). It describes the very portfolio website the user is currently viewing, with a recursive-looking infinite-loop icon linking back to `https://geekip.dev`.
*   **Box 7 (The Violet Ray - Connections):** The final ray culminates in a violet, ultra-violet glowing hub. Instead of an elongated text box, this is a sleek, horizontal control panel containing large, beautifully designed SVG icons. The purple light splits three ways to illuminate icons for GitHub (`github.com/gevak`), LinkedIn (`linkedin.com/in/gevakip`), and Facebook (`facebook.com/geva.kipper`). Hovering over each icon triggers a localized violet flare.

**Footer & the AI Timekeeper**
At the very bottom of the page, the seven colored beams merge back together into a single point, fading into absolute black. Floating in the negative space is the footer, designed to look like a heads-up display (HUD). Rendered in a crisp, monospaced tech font, it reads: "Concept and design generated by an AI model." 
Directly below this is a live, ticking JavaScript stopwatch interface. It is styled with a glowing, holographic aesthetic, displaying the real-time calculation of Hours, Minutes, and Seconds elapsed since a fixed point in spacetime: `2026-04-08 01:51:49`. The numbers should pulse subtly with every passing second, completing the ever-changing, futuristic aesthetic of the portfolio.
