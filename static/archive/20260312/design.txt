The website will be styled as a single, continuous roll of thermal receipt paper spooling dynamically down the center of a dark, minimalist viewport. The background should be a sleek, matte carbon-grey, reminiscent of a modern checkout scanner bed. The receipt paper itself is slightly off-white (#f9f9f9) with a very subtle crumpled paper texture to give it a tactile feel, and its top and bottom edges feature a jagged, torn-paper CSS effect. The main typography uses a crisp monospace font (like JetBrains Mono or VT323) with varying weights of dark, faded grey and stark black to emulate thermal printer ink.

Upon page load, the website features a "printing" animation: the receipt slides down from a dark slot at the top of the screen, with each line of text appearing incrementally accompanied by a slight, mechanical stutter effect. As the user scrolls down, the paper seamlessly extends, mimicking a continuous print job. Hovering over any interactive link triggers a bright, horizontal red "laser line" animation to pan across the text, mimicking a grocery barcode scanner, accompanied by the text briefly glowing high-contrast red.

The top "header" of the receipt acts as the store's storefront information. It is center-aligned and features a stylish, ASCII-art-inspired logo of the initials "GK". Below this, bold text reads "GEVA KIPPER", followed by a line formatting his role as a store branch: "CURRENTLY: SOFTWARE ENGINEER @ GOOGLE". Separated by a line of dashed characters (`- - - - - - - - - -`), the AI-generated subtitle is printed in uppercase: "NOTICE: THIS WEBSITE'S DESIGN IS RE-GENERATED DAILY BY AI. EXPECT AN EVER-CHANGING INVENTORY."

The main body of the receipt consists of the six projects, formatted as itemized store purchases. Each "item" occupies its own block, left-aligned with a dashed border separating it from the next. The title of the project acts as the purchased item (e.g., `01 [GLGLSTATS] ................ 1X PYTHON`), followed by an indented, word-wrapped description. Links within these sections are styled as attached "Coupons" or "Warranty Info" situated directly below the description. 
Item 1 features the LZ77 pop-chart repetitiveness description alongside bolded "[VISIT SITE]" and "[WATCH PYDATA TLV PRESENTATION]" link-tags. 
Item 2, 'Ahuzat Dibuk', outlines the Tel-Aviv parking heatmap scraping project, with its external links styled as "[MEDIA: CH13]", "[MEDIA: MAKO]", and "[SOCIAL: FACEBOOK PUBLICATION]". 
Item 3, '2D Part Orienting', details the C++ computational geometry push-plan algorithm, offering a "[SOURCE CODE & INFO]" link. 
Item 4, 'The Turing Jest', describes the LMSYS-like LLM pun arena, followed by a "[PLAY JEST ARENA]" link. 
Item 5, 'Rhymes of Israel', explains the comedic yearbook-style news headline Chrome extension, featuring an "[INSTALL EXTENSION]" link. 
Item 6 acts as a meta-purchase titled "PORTFOLIO WEBSITE", noting its self-referential nature with a link pointing back to geekip.dev.

The bottom portion of the receipt serves as the footer and payment summary. Section 7 is styled as a "LOYALTY MEMBER PROFILE", placing the GitHub, LinkedIn, and Facebook links side-by-side in a table-like format under a "CONNECT SCANNERS" header. Below this sits a visually striking, scannable CSS-generated barcode graphic stretching across the width of the receipt. Beneath the barcode is the metadata: a line reading "CASHIER ID: AI LANGUAGE MODEL", fulfilling the credit requirement. Finally, the total transaction time is displayed via a live Javascript stopwatch labeled "TIME SINCE LAST SYSTEM UPDATE (2026-03-12 01:22:44)". Its numbers tick upward dynamically, styled as a glowing LCD-style font embedded within the thermal paper, providing a stark, elegant contrast to the rest of the monochrome design before the paper terminates in a jagged tear.
