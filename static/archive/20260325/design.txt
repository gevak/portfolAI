**Global Layout & Aesthetic**
The website operates as a full-viewport, single-page interactive experience styled as a deep-space solar system. The aesthetic is elegant, dark-mode glassmorphism. The background is a highly polished infinite starfield with a subtle CSS-driven parallax dust effect. Instead of a traditional vertical scroll, navigation functions via CSS/JS zooming and clicking on rotating celestial bodies. The central focal point is a radiant, pulsing "Sun" acting as the profile header, surrounded by concentric, semi-transparent orbital rings along which the seven data points revolve. 

**The Central Star (Header & Identity)**
At the dead center of the screen is a glowing, stylized solar core. Inside or seamlessly superimposed over it in crisp, sans-serif typography is the developer's name: **Geva Kipper**.
Immediately below the name is an elegant, glowing sub-header: *Software Engineer at Google, engineering scalable platforms and impactful digital infrastructure.* 
Orbiting this textual core in a tight, fast ring is the fluid subtitle: *This universe is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing.*

**The Orbiting Planets (Interactive Project Modules)**
There are seven distinct celestial bodies orbiting the center at varying speeds and distances. Hovering over a planet halts its orbit. Clicking it triggers a smooth JavaScript zoom that brings the planet to the center of the viewport, fading in a sleek glass-pane "Data Card" to its right containing the project details and links.

*   **Planet 1: GlglStats (The Python Gas Giant)**
    *   *Visual:* A blue and yellow gas giant (Python colors) with pulsating soundwave-like rings representing LZ77 compression and pop-music beats. 
    *   *Card Content:* Text explaining the project's measurement of repetitiveness in Israeli pop-charts via compression algorithms. It features two glowing, holographic buttons: one launching the [Live Site (https://glglstats.geekip.dev)] and another with a play icon for the [PyData TLV Presentation (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo)].
*   **Planet 2: Ahuzat Dibuk (The Terrestrial Heat-Map)**
    *   *Visual:* A rocky, dark terrestrial planet covered in glowing localized patches of red, orange, and yellow, perfectly mimicking the Tel-Aviv parking occupancy heat-maps. 
    *   *Card Content:* Details the Python scraping and data visualization project for residents. Below the text, three floating beacon-like icons link outwardly to the news coverage on [Channel 13], [Mako], and the original [Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post].
*   **Planet 3: 2D Part Orienting (The Crystalline Polygon)**
    *   *Visual:* Unlike the spherical planets, this is an ever-shifting, 3D geometric C++ crystal. Its shape constantly rearranges into different complex polygons.
    *   *Card Content:* Describes the C++ open-source computational geometry implementation for algorithmic "push plans." Features an academic-styled portal link leading to the [TAU CGL Project Page].
*   **Planet 4: The Turing Jest (The Swirling Anomaly)**
    *   *Visual:* An erratic, vibrant, nebula-like planet representing LLM chaos, subtly featuring comedy and tragedy theater masks etched into its atmospheric swirls.
    *   *Card Content:* Pitch for the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete at making and explaining puns. A prominent, glowing "Enter the Arena" button links to [https://jest.geekip.dev].
*   **Planet 5: Rhymes of Israel (The Silver Satellite)**
    *   *Visual:* Modeled as an artificial metallic satellite—representing the Chrome browser ecosystem—orbiting the system, coated in a texture resembling a high school yearbook page.
    *   *Card Content:* Explains the LLM Chrome extension that dynamically rewrites Israeli news headlines into comedic rhymes. Includes an official-looking "Install Chrome Extension" button linking to the [Chrome Web Store].
*   **Planet 6: The Meta-Portfolio (The Mirror Planet)**
    *   *Visual:* A perfectly smooth, chrome mirror-planet that reflects the rest of the solar system design around it—a visual pun on the recursion of building a portfolio about building a portfolio.
    *   *Card Content:* A cheeky, self-aware blurb ("A portfolio website project... how meta!") with an infinite-loop symbol linking back to [https://geekip.dev].

**Outer Orbit Space Station (Section 7: Socials)**
*   *Visual:* On the furthest, slowest orbital ring rests a highly detailed, futuristic orbital space station rather than a planet. This serves as the communications array. 
*   *Card Content:* When clicked, the station expands a terminal dashboard featuring sleek, interconnecting nodes acting as links to [GitHub], [LinkedIn], and [Facebook].

**The Command Console Footer**
Fixed at the absolute bottom of the viewport is a sleek, black, translucent command bar representing a spaceship's heads-up display. 
*   *Left side alignment:* "Stellar architecture and UI generated by AI model." 
*   *Right side alignment:* A glowing red, monospaced digital stopwatch constantly ticking up, labeled "System uptime since 2026-03-25 01:31:05:" dynamically displaying live **[HH], [MM], [SS]** via Javascript.
