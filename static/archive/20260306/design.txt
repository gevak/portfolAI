**Overall Layout and Aesthetic Concept**
The overarching design utilizes a "Cybernetic Glassmorphism" aesthetic set against an immersive, deep-space gradient background (`#0B0D17` to `#1A1A2E`). The screen serves as a single-page atmospheric canvas. At the center of the viewport hovers a fully playable, interactive 3D Rubik's Cube built via WebGL/Three.js intertwined with CSS3D. Floating ambient dust particles and subtle neon lighting hit the cube dynamically. Surrounding the centralized cube is a minimalist, heads-up-display (HUD) layout that houses text identities, structural details, and navigational metadata.

**Header and Top Typography (HUD Left & Right)**
Fixed at the top-left of the screen, rendered in crisp, geometric typography (like Inter or Space Grotesk), is the developer’s name: "Geva Kipper". Immediately beneath it is the first subtitle in a slightly muted silver: "Software Engineer at Google, currently building and scaling high-performance infrastructure." Below this, highlighted by a gently pulsing neon border, is the second subtitle: "Dynamic Framework Active: This website's design is hallucinated and re-rendered daily by AI, resulting in an ever-changing digital footprint." 

**Core Interaction: The 3D Rubik's Cube**
The central 3x3x3 Rubik's Cube features 6 main faces, perfectly aligning with the six portfolio projects. Each face comprises 9 smaller square "stickers". When a face is on the same plane, these 9 stickers form a continuous, high-resolution composite graphic representing the project. 
The user can drag the background to rotate the entire cube and observe all sides, or drag specific rows/columns of the cube to actually "play" it. Mousing over any specific face causes its 9 tiles to extrude slightly outward on the Z-axis, emitting a soft glow. Double-clicking a face triggers a transition: the cube locks, rotates to center the chosen face to the camera, and the 9 tiles seamlessly break apart and expand outward into a flat, readable 2D glass pane containing the project's description and active hyperlinks.

**The Six Cube Faces (Projects 1-6)**
*   **Face 1 (GlglStats):** Color-coded in Neon Pink and Cyan. The composite graphic is a stylized soundwave morphing into an LZ77 data compression grid. When expanded, it presents "GlglStats: Measuring Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness." It features two prominent buttons: a link to the live app (`https://glglstats.geekip.dev`) and an embedded thumbnail linking to the PyData TLV YouTube lecture (`https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`).
*   **Face 2 (Ahuzat Dibuk):** Color-coded in Heatmap gradients (Deep Blue to Crimson). The background art is an abstract, glowing street map of Tel Aviv overlaid with density blobs. The expanded view describes the Python scraping and parking occupancy visualizer. It features a modern media dock containing linked icons for the coverage on Channel 13, Mako, and the original Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook post.
*   **Face 3 (2D Part Orienting):** Color-coded in Monochromatic Wireframe (Neon Yellow on Slate). The graphic shows a 2D geometric polygon trapped between algorithmic mathematical “push rails”. The expanded view explains the C++ open-source comp-geo library implementation, with a prominent terminal-style link to the TAU CS project page (`https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/`).
*   **Face 4 (The Turing Jest):** Color-coded in Royal Purple and Gold. The visual is an AI microchip wearing classical comedy/tragedy theater masks. The flat view details the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs battle over puns. It contains a glowing "Enter Arena" button linking to `https://jest.geekip.dev`.
*   **Face 5 (Rhymes of Israel):** Color-coded in Retro Sepia and Pastel Blue. The graphic merges pixelated newsprint with a nostalgic high-school yearbook layout. The text highlights the LLM-powered Chrome extension formatting news headlines into comedic rhymes, accompanied by an "Available in Chrome Web Store" badge linking to the extension page.
*   **Face 6 (Portfolio / Meta):** Color-coded in Iridescent / Holographic White. The imagery is a recursive, infinite-mirror view of the Rubik's cube itself, floating inside lines of code. The text explains the recursive "meta" nature of the current site natively generated. It holds a self-referencing hyper-portal link back to `https://geekip.dev`.

**The Holographic Base (Section 7: Social Links)**
Because the Rubik's cube dictates a strict 6-face geometry, the 7th requested section is placed natively inside the 3D environment as a sleek, glowing pedestal located directly beneath the hovering cube. This rotating metallic ring features three striking, 3D holographic icons. Hovering over the pedestal interacts with these items, revealing elegant tooltip lines connecting to: `github.com/gevak`, `linkedin.com/in/gevakip`, and `facebook.com/geva.kipper`.

**Footer and Temporal HUD Elements**
Fixed to the bottom-left of the viewport is the AI credit line, formatted in a monospace accent font: `[ARCHITECTURE_NODE: Designed & Generated by AI]`. On the exact opposite side, at the bottom-right corner, is a digital stopwatch. It utilizes Javascript to calculate real-time synchronization, displaying the exact time elapsed since the precise timestamp. It reads `T-ELAPSED SINCE [2026-03-06 01:28:53]:` followed by a constantly ticking `HH:MM:SS` display in a bright, glowing green, grounding the ever-changing interface in strict chronological reality.
