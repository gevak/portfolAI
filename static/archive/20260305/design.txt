The website is designed as a full-screen, interactive, panning and zooming digital canvas modeled after a modern, futuristic subway map. The aesthetic is "dark mode transit system," featuring a deep slate background (#0f172a) with subtle, animated grid lines. Instead of traditional scrolling, the user views an SVG-based network of brightly glowing neon transit lines (e.g., the "Data Express," the "AI Loop," the "Algorithms Line") that intersect at major hubs. 

In the top-left corner, a sleek, glassmorphic floating overlay card acts as the "Central Terminal" header. It displays the name **Geva Kipper** in a bold, modern sans-serif typeface, followed by the sentence: "Software Engineer at Google, building and scaling advanced technological solutions." Below this, a glowing amber subtitle reads: *"System Status: This website is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing."*

The core content is distributed across seven primary "Transfer Stations" mapped across the canvas. Initially, these appear as stylized circular transit nodes with pulsating halos representing the technologies (Python, C++, LLMs) connecting them. When the user hovers over or clicks a node, an elegant, translucent informational placard expands out seamlessly, revealing the project details with crisp typography and interactive elements:

**Station 1: GlglStats Terminal** sits on the "Data Line." The pop-out card describes the Python project that measures the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs using LZ77 compression and visualizes the patterns. It features two distinct transit-ticket-styled buttons: one linking to the live application (https://glglstats.geekip.dev) and another adorned with a play icon linking to the PyData TLV conference presentation (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo).

**Station 2: Ahuzat Dibuk Interchange** connects data visualization and web scraping. The panel details the Python-based project collecting Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy statistics and generating heat-maps. In the "Media Exits" section of the card, three sleek external link icons guide users to the news coverage on Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/), Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and the original viral Facebook publication (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).

**Station 3: 2D Part Orienting Hub** is positioned on the "Algorithms Line." The expanded placard presents the C++ implementation of a computational geometry algorithm designed to construct a 'push plan' for orienting polygons. A prominent "View Source & Info" button links directly to the project repository (https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/).

**Station 4: The Turing Jest Arena** serves as a major stop on the "AI Loop." It introduces the LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete at making and explaining puns, rated by users. The station features a glowing call-to-action button to enter the arena (https://jest.geekip.dev).

**Station 5: Rhymes of Israel Extension** is also located on the "AI Loop." The card describes the Chrome extension utilizing an LLM to playfully rewrite Israeli news headlines into high-school yearbook-style comedic rhymes. A stylized "Install Extension" badge directs users to the Chrome Web Store (https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb).

**Station 6: The Meta Paradox Station** acts as a self-referential loop on the map, vibrating slightly with a subtle, intentional digital glitch effect. It describes the portfolio website project itself, playfully acknowledging its own existence with a direct loop-back link to the root domain (https://geekip.dev).

**Station 7: Social Network Terminus** forms a massive interchange hub at the bottom center of the map. Instead of a traditional project card, this hub expands into three distinct, pill-shaped directional signs leading outward. Under the banner of "Connect," the signs link exactly to GitHub (github.com/gevak), LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and Facebook (facebook.com/geva.kipper).

Anchored fixed to the bottom-right corner of the viewport is a minimalist, heads-up-display (HUD). Rendered in a crisp, neon-green monospace font, it contains the final technical flourish: a credit tag reading *"Architected by ChatGPT"* followed directly by an active, ticking stopwatch continuously calculating and displaying the exact elapsed time (in hours, minutes, and seconds) since the timestamp **2026-03-05 01:26:52**, adding a striking localized sci-fi element to the dynamic AI-driven theme.
