The website features an elegant, single-page dark-mode layout that blends minimalist glassmorphism with a central, interactive 3D-isometric pyramid. The background is a fluid, slowly morphing WebGL gradient—shifting in hues of deep obsidian, electric blue, and purple—visually reinforcing the ever-changing, AI-generated nature of the site. As the user scrolls, the pyramid scales and rotates slightly, inviting interaction. The overarching theme maps Geva’s projects vertically, transitioning from foundational algorithmic and data skills at the wide base, up to advanced AI, interactive logic, and finally meta-awareness at the apex.

At the very top, floating above the pyramid, the header is typography-focused and elegant. Rendered in a sleek, modern sans-serif typeface, it introduces "Geva Kipper" in bold, glowing letters. Directly beneath is the sentence: "Software Engineer at Google, solving complex scale and infrastructure challenges," (or the exact provided equivalent). A constantly shimmering, typewriter-effect subtitle sits below this, stating: "This website is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." 

The core of the site is the Maslow’s Hierarchy Pyramid, built out of eight crystalline, clickable blocks that glow upon hover and smoothly expand via CSS transitions into detailed cards. The base tier, representing the "Physiological" foundation of core logic and data, consists of three wide blocks. The first block houses '2D Part Orienting,' featuring a subtle repeating CSS animation of a polygon being oriented. It contains a description of the C++ algorithm implementation for the open-source computational geometry library, alongside a button linking to cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/. The second foundational block presents 'GlglStats' with a visual backdrop of LZ77 compression heatmaps. It details the Python pop-chart rhythm project and includes links to glglstats.geekip.dev and the YouTube PyData presentation. The third base block is 'Ahuzat Dibuk,' stylized with a faint map grid of Tel-Aviv. It explains the Python scraping and data visualization for parking occupancy, featuring linked icons bridging out to the Channel 13 and Mako news stories, as well as the original Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post.

The second tier, representing the "Esteem and Cognition" level characterized by advanced AI and interactive applications, features three slightly narrower blocks. One block contains 'The Turing Jest,' utilizing an animated background of two chat bubbles sparring. It explains the LMSYS-like arena for LLM pun battles and links to jest.geekip.dev. Beside it is 'Rhymes of Israel,' decorated with a stylistic mix of a high school yearbook and a newspaper. It describes the LLM Chrome extension that converts news into comedic rhymes, including a direct call-to-action button to the Chrome Web Store. The third block in this tier is 'Hatul Basak,' featuring a faint, digital cat silhouette. It explains the Hebrew Wikipedia-based trivia game, its AI-generated daily challenges, and user-curated puzzles, providing a link to hatulbasak.geekip.dev.

The peak of the pyramid transitions into "Self-Actualization" and connectivity. The penultimate block rests near the top, titled 'Project Portfolio.' Playing into the meta-concept, this block's background uses an CSS "infinite mirror" visual effect, explaining that the site itself is the project, and includes a self-referential link out to geekip.dev. The glowing capstone—the apex of the pyramid—is the social and connection tier. It features glowing, minimalist vector icons for GitHub, LinkedIn, and Facebook arranged in a rotating carousel, linking respectively to github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper.

At the absolute bottom of the page, a minimalist footer anchors the experience. It includes a stylized credit line stating: "Design and Architecture generated by AI Model." Beside this text sits a sleek digital stopwatch ticking in real-time, displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds elapsed since the temporal anchor of 2026-05-03 02:12:17, achieved through a lightweight JavaScript counter matching the site's neon-accented typography.
