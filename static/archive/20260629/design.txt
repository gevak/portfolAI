**Overall Concept & Layout**
The website operates as a full-screen, immersive cinematic experience styled after the digital Heads-Up Display (HUD) and optics of a high-end mirrorless camera viewfinder. The background represents the deep, dark void inside a camera body, illuminated by subtle, concentric glass reflections (lens flare effects). A fine, minimalist white reticle (crosshairs, corner brackets, and rule-of-thirds gridlines) overlays the entire screen. The user’s scroll wheel and mouse movements act as the mechanical "focus ring" and "aperture control," dynamically manipulating the depth of field (using CSS backdrop filters and heavy blurring) to bring different project elements from an unrecognizable blurry bokeh into razor-sharp focus. 

**Header & Viewfinder Typography**
Anchored at the top center of the viewfinder overlay is a sleek, glowing monospace layout. It reads down like camera metadata: "GEVA KIPPER," followed by a clean, single sentence: "Currently building the future of software at Google." Just below this, mimicking a blinking "AUTO-ISO" or "AI-SERVO" tracking tag, is the subtitle: "ENVIRONMENT STATUS: Re-designed each day by AI. Lighting, focus, and structure are ever-changing." 

**The Focus Track (Projects 1-4)**
The portfolio items are treated as 3D physical objects suspended at different "focal lengths" within the lens. As the user scrolls, a satisfying mechanical clicking sound effect plays, and an SVG aperture blade animation opens around the currently focused project box. 
1. **GlglStats:** Focused at the closest distance, this box uses a graphical audio-waveform aesthetic. It features an embedded sleek video player (pulling from YouTube: `jrKAtOsj1Lo`) and a shimmering data-stream link to `https://glglstats.geekip.dev`, explaining the LZ77 Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness analysis.
2. **Ahuzat Dibuk:** Positioned slightly deeper, this box glows with the aesthetic of a thermal imaging map (representing the TLV parking heat-maps). It features three "Radar Target" styled external links pointing to Channel 13 (`https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/`), Mako (`https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm`), and the Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook post (`https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/`).
3. **2D Part Orienting:** Floating further back, styled as sharp, geometric C++ wireframes that resolve into a solid polygon upon focusing. It contains technical, blueprint-style typography describing the computational geometry algorithm and providing a clean link to `https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/`.
4. **The Turing Jest:** Presented as a dual-pixel autofocus interface (split screen), comparing two glowing nodes representing the LMSYS-style LLM pun competition, with an interactive-looking 'Vote' button that serves as the entry link to `https://jest.geekip.dev`.

**The Focus Track (Projects 5-8)**
As the focus shifts to the background elements, the foreground heavily blurs. 
5. **Rhymes of Israel:** Displayed within a simulated retro-Polaroid frame inside the futuristic lens, creating a humorous contrast. It mimics an Israeli highschool yearbook entry overlaid on a news ticker, featuring a distinct, clickable Chrome Webstore logo linking to `https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb`.
6. **Hatul Basak:** Designed as an interconnected web of Wikipedia-style data nodes that the "camera" pans across. The central, sharpest node explains the AI-generated catfishing.net trivia port and contains a bright, pulsing link to play and create puzzles at `https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev`.
7. **The Meta Portfolio:** An elegant, mind-bending "Droste effect"—a glossy, microscopic lens reflecting the entire website back at the user. Accompanying text notes the self-referential nature of the project with a link to `https://geekip.dev`.
8. **Social Dial:** Unlike the other boxes, this sits in the bottom right corner of the HUD, styled like a rotating physical camera mode dial. The standard P/S/A/M letters are replaced by icons for GitHub, LinkedIn, and Facebook. Hovering over them snaps the dial to active, linking respectively to `github.com/gevak`, `linkedin.com/in/gevakip`, and `facebook.com/geva.kipper`.

On the bottom right, replicating a video recording timer with a flashing red dot, is a live JavaScript stopwatch actively ticking upwards, displaying the exact time elapsed (Hours:Minutes:Seconds:Milliseconds) since the timestamp `2026-06-29 02:48:34`.
