**Overall Architecture & Visual Aesthetic**
The website is a fullscreen, single-page WebGL-driven interactive canvas that acts as a digital planetarium. The aesthetic is deep, elegant space: a palette of obsidian black, deep indigo, and shimmering stardust. Navigation breaks away from traditional scrolling; instead, the user pans, clicks, and zooms through an immersive 2D/3D galaxy map. 

UI elements float over the canvas as minimalist, frosted-glass HUD (Head-Up Display) panels, utilizing CSS `backdrop-filter` for a blurred, sleek look. Typography should blend elegant, modern sans-serifs (like Inter or Space Grotesk) with monospace fonts for telemetry elements.

**Header Overlay (Top-Left HUD)**
Anchor the persistent introductory panel to the top-left portion of the screen. 
*   **Main Title:** "Geva Kipper", rendered in a crisp, glowing white font. 
*   **Google Subtitle:** Directly below, add the sentence: "Software Engineer currently building the future at Google." 
*   **Dynamic Subtitle:** Below that, feature a slightly shimmering or subtly glitching text: "This universe is reborn daily: re-designed by AI, ever-changing, never static."

**The Galaxy Map & The Project "Boxes"**
The core interaction lies in navigating the galaxy. Seven major "Stars" populate the center viewport, each representing a project. They pulsate uniquely. When a user clicks a star, the camera smoothly zooms in, and an elegant, glowing bounding-box (HTML modal panel) expands beside it to display the project details. 

**Star System 1: GlglStats**
*   **Visual:** A star radiating sonic-wave pulses.
*   **Box Design:** The panel explains the Python/LZ77 Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness project and displays visualizations of song repetition patterns. 
*   **Actionable Elements:** Include a primary sleek button linking to `https://glglstats.geekip.dev` and a secondary button styled with a play icon linking to the PyData TLV presentation on YouTube (`https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`).
