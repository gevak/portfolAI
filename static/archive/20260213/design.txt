# Recipe Cookbook Portfolio ‚Äî Design Description

## Overall Concept & Atmosphere

The entire single-page site is designed to look and feel like a weathered, well-loved recipe cookbook lying open on a wooden kitchen counter. The background is a seamless, warm photo-texture of butcher-block wood grain, slightly blurred at the edges with a subtle vignette. Layered on top of that is a central "page" area ‚Äî a cream/off-white parchment texture with faint coffee-ring stains, tiny splatter marks, and a barely-visible grid pattern reminiscent of a notebook. The page has a soft torn-edge effect along its left and right borders and a subtle drop shadow lifting it off the wooden counter. The overall color palette is warm: cream, burnt sienna, paprika red, olive green, saffron gold, and dark espresso brown for text.

## Typography & Decorative Elements

The primary heading font is a hand-drawn serif that looks like it was written in marker on a recipe card ‚Äî something like "Playfair Display" or "Lora" for elegance with a "Caveat" or "Kalam" handwriting font used for annotations, side-notes, and the subtitle. Body text uses a clean serif like "Merriweather" at comfortable size. Scattered around the margins of the page are small, hand-drawn SVG illustrations of cooking utensils ‚Äî a whisk, a wooden spoon, a knife, a pot ‚Äî but on closer inspection each utensil is subtly morphed into a tech object: the whisk handle is a USB connector, the spoon bowl is a terminal cursor, the knife blade has binary digits etched into it, the pot has angle-brackets for handles. These are drawn in a single-stroke, warm brown ink style and float gently with a slow CSS parallax wobble when the user scrolls.

## Project Sections ‚Äî The Recipe Cards

The six project sections are presented as individual **recipe cards** arranged in a two-column, three-row masonry-style grid on the parchment page. Each card is a slightly off-white rectangle with rounded corners, a very subtle rotation (alternating between -1¬∞ and +1¬∞ per card, giving a scattered-on-the-counter feel), and a pin or piece of tape at the top ‚Äî rendered as a small SVG of a strip of washi tape in varying warm colors (paprika, saffron, olive, etc.) that appears to hold the card to the page. Each card has a soft box-shadow as if it's physically resting on the parchment.

Each recipe card follows this internal structure from top to bottom:

**Card header:** A "recipe name" in the hand-drawn serif font, bold, acting as the project title. Next to it, a small icon/emoji that represents the "cuisine type" (described per card below). Below the name, a single line in small caps and olive-green color reads the "cuisine category" ‚Äî a playful tech-cooking genre.

**"Ingredients" section:** A short bulleted list styled to look like a handwritten ingredient list (using the handwriting font, with small checkbox squares next to each item). These list the key technologies, tools, or concepts used.

**"Cooking Instructions" section:** A paragraph in the clean serif body font describing what the project does ‚Äî written in the voice of a recipe. For example, instead of dry technical language, it says things like "Fold in the compression algorithm gently until the repetition patterns rise to the surface."

**"Serving Suggestions" section:** This is where the external links live. Each link is styled as a small button that looks like a handwritten label tag ‚Äî a rounded rectangle with a dashed border, the link text in the handwriting font, and a small relevant icon to its left (a globe for websites, a play-triangle for video, a newspaper for news articles, a Facebook logo for Facebook links, a puzzle piece for Chrome extensions). On hover, each tag lifts slightly (translateY and increased shadow) and the dashed border becomes solid, with a warm color fill sliding in from left to right.

The seconds cause a tiny tick animation on a small indicator arrow on the dial's rim. Below the timer, in faint handwriting: *"Time since this cookbook was first opened."*

## Animations & Interactions

When the page first loads, the recipe cards animate in with a staggered entrance ‚Äî each card slides up from below and fades in with a 100ms delay between them, as if being dealt onto the table. The header cartouche fades in first with a gentle scale-up. A very subtle CSS animation makes the coffee-ring stains on the parchment background pulse in opacity extremely slowly, giving the page an almost imperceptibly alive quality. Scrolling is smooth. On mobile, the recipe cards stack into a single column, each card taking full width, and the slight rotations are removed for cleaner reading. The wooden background becomes less prominent and the parchment fills more of the viewport. All link hover effects are replaced with active/tap states.
