**Global Visual Motif & Atmosphere**
The website is rendered as an interactive, digital adaptation of a 1702 English newspaper broadside (akin to *The Daily Courant*). The background should feature a subtle, high-resolution CSS texture of aged, slightly foxed parchment with uneven, deckled edges fading into a dark matte background. The color palette is strictly off-white (parchment) and faded charcoal/dark brown ink. Typography is paramount: use an ornate Blackletter or Fraktur font for the masthead, a dense and traditional serif (like Caslon or Garamond) for the body text, and elaborate, illustrated drop-caps for the first letter of each section. The layout utilizes a dense CSS Grid or Masonry design, divided by thick and thin solid ink lines (borders) of varying weights. To merge 1702 aesthetics with modern interactivity, hover states on articles should trigger a subtle ink-bleeding animation or an "ink darkening" effect, making the hovered section appear freshly printed, alongside a slight magnifying glass transformation (CSS scale).
