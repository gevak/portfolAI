**Visual Theme and Core Interaction Mechanics**
The website utilizes a cinematic, first-person "Night Drive" aesthetic, blending modern dark-mode elegance with subtle retro-futuristic synthwave touches. The background is a pitch-black sky blending into a dark asphalt highway with glowing cyan lane dividers zooming past. The core interaction is scroll-linked 3D animation: scrolling down does not move the page vertically in the traditional sense, but instead propels the user's "camera" forward down the highway using CSS 3D transforms and parallax. The six portfolio sections act as colossal, glowing neon billboards that emerge from the distant vanishing point at the center of the screen, scaling up and panning to alternating sides of the road as the user drives past them.

**The Starting Line (Hero Section)**
Upon loading, the user looks through a sleek, minimalist digital dashboard layout. Spanning the entire highway like a massive, glowing toll-route gantry is the primary header. The developer's name, **Geva Kipper**, is rendered in brilliant, thick neon white. Suspended below it is the sub-header in a sleek, sans-serif font: "Software Engineer driving innovation at Google." Fading in and out like a flickering digital traffic warning sign is the dynamic subtitle: "System Alert: This digital landscape is procedurally re-designed daily by AI. Expect shifting realities."

**Billboard 1: GlglStats (Approaching on the Right)**
As the scroll/drive begins, the first billboard scales into view on the right shoulder of the highway. It is framed by a pulsing, equalizer-style neon border. The headline reads "GlglStats" in vibrant magenta. The typography pairs with a dynamic visualization graphic mimickingLZ77 compression patterns inside pop-chart songs. An embedded, autoplaying but muted YouTube video of the PyData TLV presentation is seamlessly integrated into the billboard's surface as a digital jumbo-tron. Below the video, a glowing, clickable neon road-sign directs traffic to https://glglstats.geekip.dev.

**Billboard 2: Ahuzat Dibuk (Approaching on the Left)**
The next structure is a high-tech grid floating on the left. Its background is a faint, animated thermal heatmap representing Tel-Aviv parking occupancy, shifting slowly between deep reds and bright greens. The text describes the Python scraping and visualization framework used by city residents. Hanging from the bottom of this billboard by rendered digital cables are three smaller, flashing neon signs acting as external link buttons, labeled "Channel 13 News," "Mako Magazine," and "Secret Tel Aviv."

**Billboard 3: 2D Part Orienting (Approaching on the Right)**
This billboard ditches the traditional rectangular shape for a sharp, wireframe polygon that slowly rotates in 3D space, illuminated in electric green. The text details the C++ computational geometry implementation and push-plan algorithm. A sleek, blueprint-styled schematic acts as the link to the Tel Aviv University CGL project page, glowing brightly when the user's cursor hovers over it.

**Billboard 4: The Turing Jest (Approaching on the Left)**
Designed to resemble a retro arcade fighter marquee or a glowing cyberpunk comedy club sign, this board is visually split down the middle. One side glows red, the other blue, representing the LLM combat arena where models battle using puns. A massive, flashing neon button reading "ENTER THE ARENA" links directly to https://jest.geekip.dev.

**Billboard 5: Rhymes of Israel (Approaching on the Right)**
This section juxtaposes old and new: a billboard styled like a classic high school yearbook page, but rendered with illuminated amber backlighting. The description outlines the Chrome extension that uses LLMs to satirize Israeli daily news headlines into comedic rhymes. A realistic, glowing "Add to Chrome Web Store" badge serves as the primary call-to-action link.

**Billboard 6: The Meta-Portfolio (Overhead Archway)**
As the drive reaches its climax, the user passes under a unique structure: an archway spanning the entire road that utilizes a CSS "infinity mirror" effect, reflecting the digital highway back onto itself. This is the portfolio's self-reference point. The bold text calls out the meta-nature of presenting the very site the user is scrolling through, featuring a glowing exit-ramp sign that links back to https://geekip.dev.

**The City Limits (Footer & Socials)**
The road ends as the user arrives at a neon-lit plaza overlooking a digital cyber-city skyline. Here, Box 7 is presented as three colossal, holographic monoliths planted on the ground, carrying glowing, minimalist icons and links for GitHub (github.com/gevak), LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and Facebook (facebook.com/geva.kipper). 

On the "dashboard" of the user's screen—fixed firmly to the bottom edge—the final credits appear in a crisp, green, monospace terminal font. It reads: "Architectural rendering by AI. Uptime since 2026-03-14 01:25:11: " followed by a live, ticking JavaScript stopwatch displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds elapsed.
