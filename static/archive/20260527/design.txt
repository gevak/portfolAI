The website is a single-page interactive experience structured around a "Bioluminescent Digital Spring" theme. The background begins as a deep, rich midnight blue at the top and transitions into a soft, hazy neon-emerald at the bottom. The core scrolling mechanic uses JavaScript (like GSAP's ScrollTrigger) to draw out a glowing, central "stem" that grows downward as the user scrolls. As the stem crosses the vertical threshold of the viewport, wireframe branches shoot toward the left and right, and upon completing their paths, vibrant digital "flowers" and "leaves" bloom into existence, acting as the containers for the project sections. The aesthetic marries organic shapes with cybernetic UI elements—think translucent glassmorphism petals, glowing SVG bezier curves, and dark mode containers accented with vibrant spring colors (magenta, cyan, lime green).

At the top of the page, the Hero section acts as the "sun" or "energy source" of the garden, featuring a radiating, slow-pulsing CSS radial gradient. The typography is elegant and modern, utilizing a crisp sans-serif font glowing with a soft white aura. It displays the name "Geva Kipper" in large, striking text, followed by the sentence: "Software Engineer at Google, crafting modern digital infrastructure and scalable solutions." Beneath this, presented as a delicate, cursive digital vine, is the subtitle: "This ecosystem is re-designed every day by AI, mutating and blooming into new configurations—no two days are the same." 

The content is divided into eight distinct nodes (boxes) that hang off the main stem in an alternating left-to-right zigzag layout. Instead of traditional rectangular cards, these sections are designed as glowing, semi-transparent terrariums or geometric petals that expand on hover. 

The first node, presenting 'GlglStats', blooms into a flower where the petals resemble audio spectrum bars colored in vivid pop-art gradients. The text details the Python, LZ77 compression, and Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness. Two interactive elements appear as glowing dewdrops on the leaves: a primary link to `https://glglstats.geekip.dev` and an embedded, auto-playing silent thumbnail (or a stylized lightbox link) for the PyData TLV presentation at `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`.

The second node, 'Ahuzat Dibuk', takes the shape of a geometric lotus textured like a thermal heat-map, representing the Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy data. Links to the news stories are styled as small satellite buds orbiting the lotus, directing users to Channel 13 (`https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/`), Mako (`https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm`), and the original Facebook publication (`https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/`).

The third node features '2D Part Orienting'. As the user scrolls to it, this box uses CSS transforms to "unfold" like origami, mimicking the push-plan algorithm. It visually blends C++ code snippets with sharp, polygonal computational geometry shapes. A distinct call-to-action button links to `https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/`. 

The fourth node, 'The Turing Jest', blooms as a split-colored blossom—representing two competing LLMs. The petals physically clash and overlap using hover animations. The description of the pun-explaining arena sits gracefully inside the blossom, with a primary call-to-action linking to `https://jest.geekip.dev`.

The fifth node represents 'Rhymes of Israel' and emerges as a flower made of folded newsprint textures that instantly colorize into bright, highschool yearbook-style neon doodles. The text explains the comedic LLM Chrome extension, and a prominent badge links directly to the Chrome Web Store (`https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb`).

The sixth node, 'Hatul Basak', is an eccentric, fractal-patterned orchid shaped subtly like a cat silhouette. The text highlights the Hebrew Wikipedia trivia, AI mechanics, and user-generated puzzles. The link to play is attached to a glowing pollen-spore element directing to `https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev`.

The seventh node represents the portfolio itself, creating a meta-visual loop. It takes the form of a "seed" or a recursive, miniature version of the entire page's garden, constantly spinning. The text acknowledges the meta-nature of the project, providing a self-referential link to `https://geekip.dev`.

The final node sits at the base of the stem, functioning as the roots of the garden plunging into the digital soil. This section displays 'Geva Kipper's Socials' as a cluster of three luminescent mushrooms. Hovering over each mushroom illuminates an icon and text link for GitHub (`github.com/gevak`), LinkedIn (`linkedin.com/in/gevakip`), and Facebook (`facebook.com/geva.kipper`). 

At the absolute bottom of the page, the "roots" fade into a dark, starlit ground layer. Here, displayed in a clean, monospace terminal font, is the website's footer. It includes a credit reading, "Cultivated and generated by [AI Model Name]." Next to it sits a live, ticking JavaScript stopwatch with a glowing neon outline, dynamically calculating and displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds that have passed since `2026-05-27 02:46:53`, reinforcing the fluid, time-based, and ever-changing nature of the garden.
