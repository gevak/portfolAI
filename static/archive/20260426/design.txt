The website follows a dark-mode "bento-box" dashboard layout, heavily inspired by modern financial apps and credit-monitoring portals like Credit Karma or Experian, but repurposed to measure "Programming Reliability". The color palette features deep slate grays, matte blacks, and glowing neon accents in "excellent credit" green, along with subtle data-viz purples and blues. Typography relies heavily on modern sans-serifs (like Inter or Roboto) paired with strict monospaced fonts (like Fira Code) for numbers and data labels. The page employs glassmorphism, with semi-transparent, slightly blurred widget panels resting on a very dark, subtly grid-patterned background.

At the very top, a full-width alert banner mimics a banking security notice, displaying the subtitle in a scrolling ticker or static yellow text: "NOTICE: System architecture and layout are re-designed daily by AI, resulting in an ever-changing interface." Below this is the Hero Module. Dominating the top center is a massive, glowing semi-circular gauge chart (a classic credit score arc), maxed out in the bright green zone with a high score like "850" or "999". Inside the arc, large, crisp typography reads "Geva Kipper", with a smaller tag beneath it stating: "Current Allocation: Software Engineer at Google". The arc pulses softly, making the reliability score feel live.

Below the hero, the content is organized into a dense, interlocking 8-pane masonry grid. Each project is framed as an "Assessment Factor" or "Credit Sub-score" widget.
