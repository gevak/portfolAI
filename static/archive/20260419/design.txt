**Overall Aesthetic and ASMR Integration**
The design revolves around a "Digital Zen Garden" concept, utilizing a tactile, bento-box grid layout to deliver a deeply comfortable and sensorily pleasing ASMR experience. The visual language heavily employs glassmorphism with heavy backdrop blurring, soft pillowy shadows (neumorphism), and large, generous padding to eliminate visual clutter. The background is a full-viewport canvas featuring a slow, fluid, warm-toned mesh gradient—blending hues of matcha green, warm sand, and soft lavender—that perpetually "breathes" using CSS keyframe animations. 

Audio interactions are central to the comfortable UI. A minimalist, fixed audio-control widget in the top right corner allows users to toggle ambient background sounds (options: distant soft rain, gentle sea waves, or deep brown noise). Every UI interaction triggers highly satisfying, soft ASMR micro-sounds implemented via JavaScript: hovering over a link produces a soft, low-frequency "thump" (like a custom mechanical keyboard), and clicking triggers a gentle, muted "pop" resembling water dropping into a pool. Hover states feature slow, 400ms cubic-bezier easing where boxes slightly depress inward like memory foam, increasing their border-radius and inner shadow.

**Typography and Header Section**
The typography relies on an elegant, highly legible rounded sans-serif font (like Quicksand or Nunito) for the body text, paired with a soft, modern serif (like Lora) for headers. The top of the page serves as the focal introduction area. It loudly yet softly presents the developer's name: **Geva Kipper**. Directly underneath, displayed in a soothing slate-grey, is the sentence: "Developing large-scale infrastructure and exploring the boundaries of web experiences as a Software Engineer at Google." Below this sits the subtitle in an italicized, muted tone, pulsing very gently with opacity: *This digital space is reimagined and re-designed each day by AI, making it a living, breathing, and ever-changing environment.*

**The Bento-Box Grid Layout (Eight Projects Showcase)**
The layout cascades into an asymmetric, masonry-style bento grid composed of eight tactile, frosted-glass container boxes smoothly floating over the breathing background.

**Box 1:** A spacious, wide rectangular card dedicated to **GlglStats**. The UI here integrates subtle, slow-moving wave animations in the background, mimicking audio frequencies. It details a Python project measuring the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs using LZ77 compression and visualizing the patterns of famous tracks. The card features two soft-glow buttons: one linking to the live site (https://glglstats.geekip.dev) and another linked to a YouTube video of the PyData TLV conference presentation (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo), accompanied by a custom SVG icon of a playing screen.

**Box 2:** A deep, square card featuring **Ahuzat Dibuk**. The aesthetic incorporates a heavily blurred heat-map gradient in the background. The text describes a Python scraping and data visualization project collecting Tel-Aviv car parking lot occupancy statistics to generate heat-maps for residents. It lays out elegant typographic links detailing its media coverage on Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/) and Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), alongside a hyperlink to the original viral publication on the Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook group (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).

**Box 3:** A vertical, pillar-shaped container for **2D Part Orienting**. The card illustrates geometric elegance with slowly spinning, minimal polygon SVGs in the background. The copy outlines a C++ implementation within an open-source computational geometry library, detailing an efficient algorithm designed to construct a 'push plan' to orient a given polygon. A soft, pill-shaped button anchors the bottom, pointing to the code and additional information (https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/).

**Box 4:** A sleek card presenting **The Turing Jest**. The design uses subtle, masked chatbot bubbles floating upwards as a background motif. The description explains it as an LMSYS-like arena where large language models are pitted against each other to generate and explain puns, dynamically rated by the user base. A glowing link directs users to the arena (https://jest.geekip.dev).

**Box 5:** A vibrant but soft card highlighting **Rhymes of Israel**. The aesthetic features notebook-paper-like horizontal lines rendered in high-transparency white. The text describes a Chrome extension employing an LLM to automatically rewrite Israeli news headlines into comedic rhymes, styling them like high school yearbook quotes. A button styled like a soft rubber stamp links directly to the Chrome Web Store installation page (https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb).

**Box 6:** An engaging block containing **Hatul Basak**. Behind the text, a faint, abstract illustration of a cat resting elegantly fades loosely into the glass. The content presents a Hebrew port of catfishing.net—a Wikipedia-based Trivia game. It highlights the use of AI to automatically generate daily challenges and notes the availability of user-generated, themed puzzles. The link to play and create custom puzzles is highlighted with an inviting, gently pulsating effect (https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev).

**Box 7:** A perfectly square, highly reflective card housing the **Meta Portfolio Project**. The background of this card displays an infinitely mirroring, blurred version of the webpage itself (an "infinity mirror" CSS effect). It cheekily points out the meta nature of showcasing the very site the user is exploring, containing a recursive self-reference button linking straight back home (https://geekip.dev). 

**Box 8:** The final grid item acts as a **Social Hub Box**. It mimics the physical texture of a soft, woven acoustic panel. It presents three minimalist, elegantly sculpted 3D icons that, when hovered, emit the sound of a soft brush stroking a wooden block. The icons cleanly link to the developer’s specific social networks: github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper. 

**Footer and Time-Tracking Element**
The single-page experience tapers smoothly into a minimalist footer. Centered at the bottom in a delicate micro-typography is a credit acknowledging the design was architected by an AI language model. Directly below this sits a digital stopwatch rendered with monospaced numerals styled like an old-school liquid crystal display, but glowing softly through frosted glass. Driven by JavaScript, this timer continuously counts up in hours, minutes, and seconds, visually tracking exactly how much time has passed since the anchor timestamp: `2026-04-19 02:04:48`, grounding abstract, transient AI design in concrete reality.
