The website operates purely on a Z-axis scroll mechanic, creating a visually endless "Droste effect" optical illusion. Instead of scrolling down a flat page, the user's scroll wheel drives the camera forward through a series of infinite, 3D architectural doorways. The background transitions continuously from glowing blueprint wireframes into photorealistic brutalist concrete and sleek glass, symbolizing the journey from abstract code to polished software architecture.

The initial view—the outermost doorway—features an elegant, frosted-glass monolith suspended in the center of the screen. The developer’s name, "Geva Kipper," is displayed in a crisp, modern, dimensional typography. Below it, a sleek sentence reads: "Software Engineer currently engineering scalable solutions and shaping the ecosystem at Google." Beneath this, a typewriter animation displays the subtitle along with subtle visual glitches: "This website is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." 

As the user scrolls, the camera plunges through the first doorway, taking them into eight consecutive, nested architectural chambers. Inside each chamber, the project contents are displayed on floating, minimalist displays that the user physically "passes through" via scroll. 

Chamber One houses 'GlglStats'. The walls of this doorway pulse with audio-reactive visualizers and glowing LZ77 compression patterns stretching across the architecture. The display explains the Python pop-chart repetitiveness project and includes futuristic, media-player-style buttons linking to the live site at https://glglstats.geekip.dev, as well as an embedded viewing portal linking to the PyData TLV conference presentation at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo.

Chamber Two presents 'Ahuzat Dibuk'. The architecture here shifts into a dark-mode, sprawling city grid map. The project slab is illuminated from beneath by dynamic, animated heat-maps glowing in reds and oranges to represent Tel Aviv parking occupancy. Sleek, glowing data-node links direct the user to the media stories on Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/), Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and the Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).

Chamber Three is '2D Part Orienting'. The doorway becomes an array of intersecting geometric planes. The floating display features a live, looping animation of a computational geometry polygon rotating and aligning itself against a virtual wall, representing the push-plan algorithm. A sleek button links to the C++ open-source code and additional info at https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/.

Chamber Four represents 'The Turing Jest'. This doorway takes the form of a surreal, neon-lit colosseum. The layout splits into two dueling dialogue boxes reflecting the LMSYS-like arena, bordered by glowing theatrical comedy masks. A prominently illuminated exit sign serves as the link to https://jest.geekip.dev.

Chamber Five is dedicated to 'Rhymes of Israel'. The walls of this doorway mirror a high-speed news printing press that seamlessly morphs into a vibrant, nostalgic high school yearbook layout. The typography is a chaotic but elegant mix of serious journalistic serif fonts and playful handwritten rhymes. A pulsing, 3D Chrome logo serves as the installation link to the Chrome Web Store extension (https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb).

Chamber Six houses 'Hatul Basak'. The architecture here is a sprawling web of Wikipedia-style hyperlinks interconnecting in the background to form subtle cat silhouettes. The central display explains the AI-generated trivia game and features a playful, oversized node linking to https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev where users can play or create their own puzzles.

Chamber Seven is the "meta" inception portal for the portfolio website itself. The walls of this chamber are made entirely of digital mirrors, infinitely reflecting the previous doorways and the entire website recursively. The display acknowledges the meta nature of the project with a self-referential hyper-loop link pointing back to https://geekip.dev.

Chamber Eight, the final doorway, is a sleek, hacker-centric terminal command center. Minimalist, glowing vector icons and command-line prompts provide the social links to github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper. Once the user scrolls through this final doorway, the endless loop smoothly resets, depositing them back at the hero section, though the ambient lighting of the entire site subtly shifts hue to emphasize the infinite architectural theme.

Anchored permanently to the bottom of the viewport is an elegant, semi-transparent frosted-glass HUD footer that floats above the scrolling 3D environment. The left side features a crisp typographic credit: "Architecture and design dynamically generated by an AI model." On the right side sits a live, cryptographic stopwatch in a modern monospaced cyber-font. Using JavaScript, this timer actively ticks upward, displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds elapsed since the temporal anchor of 2026-07-01 02:48:48.
