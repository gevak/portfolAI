## Hover & Interaction â€” The Dispensing Mechanic

On the right side of the vending machine (outside the glass, on the metal body), there is a **numeric keypad** rendered decoratively â€” a 3x4 grid of round buttons labeled 0-9, plus A and B, with embossed metallic styling and a subtle inset shadow. These buttons are not just decorative: when a user **hovers** over any project card inside the glass, the corresponding keypad buttons (e.g., A then 1 for slot A1) glow with a brief amber backlight. If the user **clicks** a project card, a delightful dispensing animation plays: the coil in that slot rotates, the card tips forward and "falls" downward with a slight physics-based tumble, and a **detail panel** slides up from the bottom of the vending machine (from behind the **dispensing flap** â€” a small rubber-flap graphic at the bottom of the glass area). This detail panel is a larger modal-like card that expands the project description, shows all links more prominently with full labels, and for GlglStats, embeds the YouTube video thumbnail as a clickable preview. Clicking outside or pressing a small "return" button (styled like a coin-return lever on the machine) slides the panel back down and resets the slot animation.

## Ambient Details and Polish

- **Coin slot:** On the upper right side of the machine body, a decorative coin slot with a small label that says "Insert curiosity." Purely decorative but adds charm.
- **Subtle hum:** An optional, very quiet ambient hum sound (toggleable via a small speaker icon) to simulate a vending machine compressor â€” just a 2-second looping low drone, off by default.
- **Background:** Behind the vending machine, the page background is a dark gradient with faint, blurred neon reflections on what appears to be wet asphalt â€” created with CSS gradients and a few soft radial glows in pink and blue.
- **Responsive behavior:** On mobile, the vending machine narrows and the grid becomes a single column of slots (stacked vertically), with the keypad hidden, and the dispensing interaction replaced by a simple tap-to-expand accordion. The machine still retains its metallic frame and glass panel, just in a taller, narrower "slim vending machine" form factor.
- **Typography:** The primary font is a clean geometric sans-serif (like Inter or DM Sans) for readability. The marquee uses a display font with a slight retro-tech flavor (like Orbitron or similar). The LED screen uses a monospace dot-matrix style font. Slot codes use a bold condensed monospace.
- **Scrolling:** The page ideally fits in one viewport on desktop with no scroll needed, but if content overflows, the scroll experience is smooth and the background stays fixed (the vending machine scrolls while the dark alley backdrop parallaxes subtly).
