**Global Theme & Visual Style**
The website is a single-page parallax experience styled as a dimly lit, high-end "Cyber-Speakeasy Lab." The background is deep charcoal with subtle, animated particle effects that look like rising carbonation bubbles turning into binary code (`0`s, `1`s) and syntax symbols (`{`, `}`, `>`). The primary accent colors are neon cocktail hues—glowing cyan, electric magenta, and toxic-waste lime green. Interaction elements act like fluid dynamics using CSS and Canvas; hovering over items causes them to ripple, smoke, or glow like a chemical reaction.

**Header & Hero Section**
The top viewport features a sleek stainless-steel bar counter littered with chemistry equipment—flasks, bunsen burners, and cocktail shakers. A central animation loops continuously: an animated cocktail shaker pours a luminescent liquid into an Erlenmeyer flask, causing glowing code snippets to evaporate out of the top. Suspended above the bar is an animated neon sign reading: **Geva Kipper**. 
Just right below the sign, a plaque with elegantly etched typography states: *"Currently brewing up solutions and mixing code as a Software Engineer at Google."*
Resting on the bar counter is a glowing digital chalkboard or LED ticker-tape serving as the subtitle. It reads: *"Menu changes daily: This space is re-designed every day by AI and is thus ever-changing."*

**The "Tasting Menu" (Project Portfolio)**
As the user scrolls down, the page transitions to a series of illuminated bar shelves containing eight distinct "Concoctions" (projects/sections). Each section is visually represented as a unique vessel (a cocktail glass, a beaker, or test tubes) sitting on a glowing coaster. The containers are built with CSS glassmorphism, and hovering over them triggers a hover-state animation where the liquid inside bubbles vigorously, projecting holographic text and links outward.

**Module 1: GlglStats**
Visualized as a layered Pousse-Café drink, where the distinct colored layers represent LZ77 compression patterns. The text defines it as a Python project measuring repetitiveness in Israeli pop-chart songs and visualizing their repetition patterns. Two floating neon bubbles act as buttons: one linking to the live site at `https://glglstats.geekip.dev` and the other styled as a tiny projector linking to the PyData TLV conference presentation at `https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo`.

**Module 2: Ahuzat Dibuk**
Visualized as a wide rocks glass containing a boiling red and orange liquid that mimics a thermal heat-map. The text describes it as a Python scraping and data visualization project mapping Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy. Attached to the glass are three garnish picks carrying "news clipping" labels. These function as links to the Channel 13 coverage (`https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/`), the Mako article (`https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm`), and the Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post (`https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/`).

**Module 3: 2D Part Orienting**
Visualized as a sleek lowball glass containing geometric ice cubes that mathematically align themselves into a perfect square when the user’s mouse touches the glass. The text details it as a C++ implementation in an open-source computational geometry library to construct a 'push plan' for orienting polygons. A metallic coaster underneath the glass serves as the link to `https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/`.

**Module 4: The Turing Jest**
Visualized as two interconnected test tubes with contrasting glowing chemicals (representing competing LLMs) rapidly exchanging sparks through a glass bridge. The description introduces an LMSYS-like arena where LLMs compete at making and explaining puns, rated by users. A glowing spigot at the bottom of the mechanism directs users to `https://jest.geekip.dev`.

**Module 5: Rhymes of Israel**
Visualized as an elegant chrome martini glass. Instead of liquid, a continuous stream of neon blue text pours out of it, arranging itself into rhyming couplets on a floating high school yearbook page. The text explains it is a Chrome extension utilizing LLMs to rewrite Israeli news headlines into comedic rhymes. An "Install Extension" button styled as an olive on a toothpick links to `https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb`.

**Module 6: Hatul Basak**
Visualized as a whimsical cat-shaped Florence flask filled with glowing purple liquid and floating "W" (Wikipedia) insignias. The text pitches it as a Hebrew port of catfishing.net—a Wikipedia trivia game featuring AI-generated daily challenges and user-created themed puzzles. Two interactive puzzle-piece buttons prompt the user to "Play" or "Create" at `https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev`.

**Module 7: The Meta Project**
Visualized as an impossibly tiny cocktail glass containing a curved mirror that reflects the entire webpage back at the user. The text cheekily notes: "A portfolio website project (how meta! That's the site you are building)". Clicking the mirror acts as a self-referential link to `https://geekip.dev`.

**Module 8: Social Connections**
Visualized as a glowing ultraviolet "Tip Jar" sitting at the far end of the shelf. Inside the jar are three holographic business cards suspended in a plasma field. They glow brighter upon interaction and direct traffic to github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper.

**Footer Section**
The bottom edge of the site looks like the base of complex lab machinery. Imprinted into dark brushed metal is the text: *"Formula devised, mixed, and styled by an AI Model."* Next to this credit sits a glowing orange Nixie-tube digital clock. The clock utilizes a JavaScript counter calculating real-time uptime, displaying the exact Hours, Minutes, and Seconds elapsed since a locked ignition date of `2026-04-22 01:59:31`.
