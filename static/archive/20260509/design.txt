The website is structured as a sleek, single-page, dark-mode experience built around the central metaphor of a "Chronolabe"—a futuristic, glassmorphic time machine dial. The overall aesthetic blends elegant retro-futurism with modern cyberpunk minimalism. The background is a deep obsidian (`#0A0A0A`) featuring a subtle, fluid animation of stardust or data-particles drifting in a parallax effect. 

Fixed at the top-left of the viewport is the header. It displays "Geva Kipper" in a bold, glowing, sans-serif typography (like Space Grotesk), accompanied by a crisp, muted silver subheading: "Developing the future of technology as a Software Engineer at Google." Right below this, encased in a softly pulsing, iridescent border, is the meta-subtitle written in a monospaced font: "Notice: This temporal interface is re-designed daily by AI; it remains ever-changing and fundamentally un-static."

The primary navigation and visual anchor is a massive, interactive, semi-transparent dial occupying the right half or center of the screen. Users do not scroll down a page; instead, scrolling or dragging rotates this metallic-and-neon dial with satisfying, spring-physics easing. The dial has eight distinct "temporal notches" (epochs), each representing a project or section. As a notch locks into the central focal point on the left side of the screen, the data for that era materializes inside a sleek, frosted-glass card with specific thematic ambient light bleeding into the background.

**Node 1: The Pop Compression Era (GlglStats)**
The ambiance shifts to neon magenta and cyan audio waves. The glass panel displays the GlglStats project, detailing the Python/LZ77 repetition analysis of Israeli pop-charts. Beside the descriptive text, a dynamic audio-bar visualizer reacts softly to the mouse hover. Two elegant, rounded buttons are present: one labeled "Access Data Archive" linking to `glglstats.geekip.dev`, and a secondary "play" icon button styled like a vintage viewfinder, linking to the PyData TLV presentation on YouTube.

**Node 2: The Urban Heat Era (Ahuzat Dibuk)**
The ambient glow turns a warning amber and crimson, overlaying a faint, animated topological map of Tel-Aviv. The text describes the Python scraping and data visualization of parking lot occupancies. The links are styled as holographic floating news clippings: two distinct modern icons link to the Channel 13 and Mako news stories, while a classic, glowing blue "F" icon links to the original Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook post.

**Node 3: The Geometric Construct Era (2D Part Orienting)**
The screen transitions into a deep blueprint-navy matrix grid, with a slow-spinning, glowing 3D wireframe polygon in the background. The text outlines the C++ computational geometry push-plan algorithm. A brutalist, cleanly designed hyper-link button directs the user to the TAU computational geometry open-source library and code.

**Node 4: The Theatrical Arena Era (The Turing Jest)**
A split neon motif of electric blue and bright pink illuminates the screen, invoking a digital colosseum or comedy club stage. The text introduces the LMSYS-like LLM pun arena. The link to `jest.geekip.dev` is housed inside a button that resembles a glowing, dual-faced theater mask (comedy/tragedy) blended with a microchip pattern.

**Node 5: The Scholastic Rhyme Era (Rhymes of Israel)**
The interface adopts a whimsical, high-school yearbook aesthetic superimposed over the dark theme—think torn digital paper edges and chrome-yellow typewriter typography. The text explains the LLM Chrome extension rewriting Israeli news into comedic rhymes. An oversized, stylized Chrome Web Store badge serves as the installation link, glowing with a soft, inviting yellow aura.

**Node 6: The Temporal Trivia Era (Hatul Basak)**
The lighting shifts to a mysterious, deep violet. The background displays a web of connecting nodes, reminiscent of a Wikipedia rabbit hole. The text describes the automated and user-generated AI daily trivia puzzles. The link to `hatulbasak.geekip.dev` is framed as a glowing puzzle piece, inviting the user to play and create.

**Node 7: The Recursive Era (Meta Portfolio)**
The visual theme here is an "infinity mirror," creating an illusion of recursive, glowing frames receding endlessly into the monitor. The text playfully points out the meta nature of showcasing the very portfolio site they are currently experiencing. A clean, hyper-minimalist link points inward to `geekip.dev`.

**Node 8: The Convergence Era (Social Links)**
The final notch on the dial brings the user to the "present baseline." The background clears to pure, obsidian glass. Hovering in the center of the panel are three large, exquisitely crafted, 3D holographic icons for GitHub, LinkedIn, and Facebook (`github.com/gevak`, `linkedin.com/in/gevakip`, `facebook.com/geva.kipper`). Hovering over each causes them to physically tilt and cast a colored digital shadow.

Pinned elegantly to the bottom center (or bottom right) of the fixed viewport is the footer. It features a stark, cyberpunk-style digital readout in an electric cyan monospaced font. The top line reads: "Chronolabe UI Architected by Gemini." Below it is a live, ticking stopwatch formatted as `HH:MM:SS`, accompanied by the text: "Temporal divergence since 2026-05-09 02:15:06 — [Live Timer]". The seconds physically tick backward or forward, grounding the site's entire time machine metaphor.
