The overall framework of the website features a dual-layer architecture: a static, glassmorphic UI layer hovering over a dynamic, full-screen 3D physics canvas. The aesthetic is elegant and dark, utilizing a deep "Midnight Space" gradient background (charcoal to deep indigo) with subtle volumetric lighting that illuminates the scene from the center. 

**The Static UI Elements (HUD Layer)**
At the top left corner, resting on a sleek, frosted-glass acrylic panel, is the typography. The developer's name, **Geva Kipper**, is displayed in a bold, modern sans-serif font (like Space Grotesk) in pure glowing white. Immediately below is a crisp, single sentence: *Currently engineering software solutions at Google.* 
Underneath that sits the subtitle, written in a monospaced, slightly dimmed font: *This portfolio is re-designed daily by AI; its layout, physics, and chaos are ever-changing.*

Anchored at the bottom center of the screen is another elegant frosted-glass pill. It contains the footer credit: *Concept conceptualized and hallucinated by AI*, accompanied by a glowing, neon-cyan digital stopwatch. The stopwatch actively ticks in the format HH:MM:SS, preceded by the text: *Time elapsed since 2026-07-05 02:28:06:*.

**The Interactive 3D Canvas & Gravity Mechanics**
Behind the fixed UI, the entire screen acts as a zero-gravity 3D viewing box. Eight distinct geometrical blocks represent the portfolio sections. By default, they lazily tumble and float through the space, gently bumping into one another with soft, physics-based dampening. 
The gravity is fully interactive: as the user moves their mouse around the screen, the gravitational center seamlessly shifts towards the cursor, pulling the blocks in that direction. On mobile devices, the device's gyroscope dictates the gravity, allowing the user to physically tilt their phone to tumble the blocks around the screen. Users can also click and drag any block to fling it across the viewport, watching it collide with the others.

When a user clicks on a block, the local gravity pauses. The selected block floats to the dead center of the screen, scaling up gracefully while its front face dissolves into a clean, readable content modal. A transparent overlay dims the other blocks into the background.

**The Eight Project Blocks**
Each block is beautifully rendered using materials like frosted glass, brushed metal, and neon accents to represent its specific project visually:

**1. GlglStats**
Design: A translucent purple 3D rectangular prism containing floating, neon musical notes and shifting audio visualizer bars. 
Expanded Content: Briefly details the Python/LZ77 algorithmic measurement of Israeli pop-chart repetitiveness and its pattern visualizations. Contains two sleek, pill-shaped buttons: A glowing web-link icon to *https://glglstats.geekip.dev* and a red YouTube play button linking to the PyData TLV conference presentation (*https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo*).

**2. Ahuzat Dibuk**
Design: A glowing, reddish-orange 3D cube wrapped in a subtle, shifting urban heat-map texture. 
Expanded Content: Describes the Python scraping and data visualization project for Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy. The links are styled as neat news-ticker buttons leading to the press coverage on Channel 13 (*https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/*) and Mako (*https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm*), alongside a blue Facebook icon linking to the original Secret Tel Aviv post (*https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/*).

**3. 2D Part Orienting**
Design: Breaking the cube theme, this is a complex, asymmetrical, faceted 3D polygon rendered in technical blueprint blue, glowing softly at its vertices. 
Expanded Content: Details the C++ computational geometry library implementation of the efficient push-plan algorithm. Features a prominent button containing a code-bracket icon, linking to the project and repository (*https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/*).

**4. The Turing Jest**
Design: A sleek, dual-toned block (comedic teal and punchy orange) that emits subtle digital sparks, representing competing AI models. 
Expanded Content: Explains the LMSYS-style arena for LLM pun battles and user ratings. A bold circular button acts as the portal to the arena (*https://jest.geekip.dev*).

**5. Rhymes of Israel**
Design: A white-acrylic cube wrapped with a scrolling digital news ticker on its sides and a high-school yearbook texture on its top. 
Expanded Content: Showcases the Chrome Extension that utilizes LLMs to twist Israeli news headlines into comedic rhymes. Includes a polished, high-contrast button featuring a 3D Chrome logo to install the extension (*https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb*).

**6. Hatul Basak**
Design: An intricate 3D puzzle-box textured with a subtle Wikipedia grid pattern and accented with rich gold trimming. 
Expanded Content: Describes the Hebrew catfishing.net trivia port, its daily AI-generated challenges, and the user-created puzzle ecosystem. A playful, glowing gold button invites the user to play and create (*https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev*).

**7. Meta-Portfolio Box**
Design: A holographic, mirror-polished cube that perfectly reflects the background, the UI, and the other floating blocks. Peeking inside reveals an endless infinity-mirror illusion. 
Expanded Content: A self-referential nod to the site currently being viewed. Contains a single, elegantly animated infinity-loop button linking to the domain (*https://geekip.dev*).

**8. The Social Hub**
Design: A minimalist, heavy-looking obsidian cube with an ultra-matte finish. The logos for GitHub, LinkedIn, and Facebook are deeply embossed into its faces, glowing with a soft white backlight. 
Expanded Content: Clean, oversized icons acting as robust clickable zones for GitHub (*github.com/gevak*), LinkedIn (*linkedin.com/in/gevakip*), and Facebook (*facebook.com/geva.kipper*).
