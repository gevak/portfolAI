## Window System Design
Each of the seven content boxes is rendered as a draggable, resizable window with authentic OS window chrome: title bar with colored traffic light buttons (close, minimize, maximize) on the left, drop shadows suggesting depth, and rounded corners. Windows are arranged in a cascading overlap pattern initially, each slightly offset. When hovered, windows subtly raise (increased box-shadow) and their title bars glow with accent colors unique to each project.

## Taskbar & Footer
The bottom taskbar spans the full width with a dark translucent background. On the left, a grid icon serves as an "app drawer" that highlights when hovered (non-functional but decorative). The center shows miniature window icons for each open "application" that pulse when their window is active. The right side displays the AI credit in small text: "Interface designed by Claude (Anthropic)" followed by a live-updating stopwatch showing elapsed time since 2025-11-05 01:07:10 in the format "Uptime: XXh XXm XXs" with monospace digits that flip smoothly.

## Interactive Details
Windows can be dragged by their title bars and will snap subtly to a grid. Clicking a window brings it to the front with a smooth z-index transition. Double-clicking a title bar toggles maximize/restore with fluid animations. The background features an extremely subtle scanline effect (barely visible) that scrolls continuously, enhancing the screen authenticity. Cursor changes to appropriate OS cursors (resize handles at window edges, pointer over buttons, grab over title bars).
