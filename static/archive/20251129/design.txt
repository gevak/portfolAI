# Black Friday Portfolio: "STACK OVERFLOW DEALS" Design Concept

## Overall Theme & Atmosphere

The website mimics a chaotic, high-energy Black Friday electronics store landing page‚Äîthink aggressive countdown timers, "DOORBUSTER" banners, price slash graphics, and that unmistakable retail urgency‚Äîbut selling coding skills instead of TVs. The color palette is classic Black Friday: deep matte black background, electric neon yellow (#FFE500) for highlights, hot red (#FF3131) for "sale" accents, and crisp white text. Subtle scanline effects overlay the entire page, giving it a slightly retro CRT monitor aesthetic that nods to both old-school retail displays and vintage computing.

## Header Section

A massive "door-buster" style banner stretches across the top with diagonal yellow caution stripes on the edges. The name **"GEVA KIPPER"** appears in an ultra-bold, slightly condensed sans-serif font (think Impact meets modern geometric typefaces), with a fake "crossed-out" price above it reading "~~$999,999~~" and below it "NOW: OPEN TO OFFERS." A small Google logo sits beside a single line in a typewriter font: *"Currently engineering chaos at Google."*

Directly beneath, a red ticker tape scrolls horizontally (like a stock market ticker or news crawl) containing the subtitle: *"‚ö° THIS SITE REDESIGNS ITSELF DAILY VIA AI ‚Ä¢ WHAT YOU SEE TODAY WILL NEVER EXIST AGAIN ‚Ä¢ LIMITED TIME REALITY ‚ö°"* ‚Äî it loops infinitely, creating urgency.

## Navigation & Layout

No traditional nav bar. Instead, the six project boxes are arranged in a staggered "deal cards" layout‚Äîimagine uneven retail shelf displays. On desktop, they form a loose 3x2 grid but with intentional misalignment: some cards tilted 1-2 degrees, some slightly overlapping, creating visual energy. Each card has a "price tag" hanging off its corner on a small animated string/rope graphic.

## Footer

Designed as a receipt paper strip with torn perforation edges at the top. Contains:
- "TRANSACTION GENERATED BY: [AI Model Name]" in receipt-style monospace font
- "STORE OPENED:" followed by the live stopwatch counting up from 2025-11-29 01:04:46, displayed in large LED-style red digits (like an old digital clock), showing HOURS : MINUTES : SECONDS
- A "THANK YOU FOR SHOPPING" message
- A fake barcode with "GEEKIP-2025" encoded visually

## Micro-interactions & Animations

- Cards slightly "bounce" or scale up on hover, mimicking the excitement of grabbing a deal
- The yellow highlights occasionally "flicker" like neon signs
- Price tags on cards sway gently as if hanging
- A subtle "shopping cart" icon in the corner shows "6 SKILLS IN CART" but is non-functional
- Confetti or sparkle particles occasionally burst from "DOORBUSTER" labels
- The scrolling ticker tape speeds up momentarily when hovered
- Background has very subtle falling price tags or percentage symbols, like snow

## Typography

Headlines: Ultra-bold condensed sans-serif (Bebas Neue or similar)
Body: Clean modern sans-serif (Inter or Roboto)
Accents: Monospace for "prices" and technical details (JetBrains Mono)
Occasional handwritten-style font for "LIMITED TIME!" callouts

## Responsive Behavior

On mobile, cards stack vertically as a single column, maintaining the chaotic sticker/tag overlaps. The ticker scrolls faster. The receipt footer becomes even more prominent, and the stopwatch digits grow larger as the dramatic "bottom line" of the page.
