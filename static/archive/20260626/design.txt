The website is designed as a fully interactive, top-down 2D pixel-art RPG that fills the entire browser window. The aesthetic blends classic 16-bit SNES-style environments with elegant, high-resolution modern UI elements (glassmorphism overlays, smooth CSS transitions, and crisp sans-serif typography) to ensure the portfolio feels both nostalgic and premium. The user drives the experience by moving a custom player-sprite directly through the world using arrow keys, WASD, or pathfinding click-to-walk mechanics.

Floating elegantly at the top-center of the screen is a persistent, semi-transparent "Heads Up Display" (HUD). It features the primary title: "Geva Kipper", accompanied by the sentence: "Taking on complex engineering challenges and building elegant solutions at Google." Directly beneath this is a pulsing, stylized subtitle indicating the site's dynamic nature: "Warning: The architecture of this realm shifts daily, generated and redesigned entirely by AI."

The single-page environment is a beautifully lit map—a stylized medieval tech-village—populated by unique Non-Player Characters (NPCs) and interactable objects. When the user navigates their sprite to an NPC and presses 'Space' or clicks, the game pauses, and a sleek, translucent modern dialog box slides up from the bottom of the screen. These dialog boxes serve as the "sections" of the portfolio, featuring animated text typing out the character’s dialogue alongside interactive, clickable high-res buttons for the external links. 

**Section 1: GlglStats**
The player encounters a cyber-bard sitting next to an ancient gramophone. When interacted with, the dialog box opens to explain the wizardry of "GlglStats," a Python project that uses LZ77 compression algorithms to measure and visualize the repetitiveness of Israeli pop-chart songs. Inside the dialog window, two stylized buttons are provided: one linking directly to the portal (https://glglstats.geekip.dev) and another opening an embedded viewing portal to a PyData TLV conference presentation (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo).

**Section 2: Ahuzat Dibuk**
The player meets a frantic carriage driver staring at a glowing, color-coded parchment. The dialog explains "Ahuzat Dibuk," a Python web-scraping and data visualization project that tracks Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy and generates real-time heat maps for local residents. The dialogue box offers a trio of "news scrolls" as buttons: Channel 13 News (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/), Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and the original Secret Tel Aviv tavern posting (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).

**Section 3: 2D Part Orienting**
Beside a giant, heavy stone polygon, the player finds a master stonemason. The mason explains a C++ implementation within an open-source computational geometry library, detailing an efficient algorithm designed to construct a 'push plan' to perfectly orient any given polygon. A button beneath the text reads "Consult the Geometry Tomes," linking to the project’s code and additional info (https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/).

**Section 4: The Turing Jest**
The player wanders into a small arena where two jesters are wildly gesturing at each other while an audience holds up scorecards. Engaging them opens a box detailing "The Turing Jest," an LMSYS-like battleground where Large Language Models are forced to compete by making and explaining puns, graded entirely by human users. A brightly themed button invites the user to "Enter the Arena" at https://jest.geekip.dev.

**Section 5: Rhymes of Israel**
Standing by a town bulletin board is an NPC dressed in a high-school letterman jacket over knight's armor. They introduce "Rhymes of Israel," a mystical browser artifact (Chrome extension) that utilizes an LLM to automatically rewrite Israeli news site headlines into comedic rhymes fitting a high-school yearbook. A glowing button allows users to "Equip the Chrome Artifact" (https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb).

**Section 6: Hatul Basak**
The player finds a feline oracle resting on a massive stone encyclopedia. The cat speaks telepathically through the dialog box, presenting "Hatul Basak," a Hebrew port of the Wikipedia-based trivia game catfishing.net. The oracle explains how it uses AI to generate daily knowledge challenges and allows users to forge their own themed riddles. A button reading "Consult the Oracle" directs the player to https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev.

**Section 7: The Meta Website**
In the corner of the map stands a swirling, magical mirror. Interacting with the mirror summons a clone of the player's sprite. The clone breaks the fourth wall, marveling at the very portfolio website project they are currently standing inside, praising its daily-shifting, AI-generated game architecture. A recursive link offers to restart the loop by directing to https://geekip.dev.

**Section 8: The Crossroads (Socials)**
At the northern edge of the village sits a wooden signpost. Interacting with the signpost brings up a minimalist, elegant menu functioning as the final section. It contains three distinct, icon-based paths leading out of the realm: The Code Repository (github.com/gevak), The Professional Guild (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and The Social Square (facebook.com/geva.kipper).

At the absolute bottom of the viewport, outside the bounds of the retro map and rendered in hyper-modern UI styling, sits the footer. It displays an elegant digital text display that reads: "Realm architected purely by AI." Next to it is a live, ticking digital stopwatch, dynamically rendering the exact hours, minutes, and seconds that have passed since the temporal anchor point of 2026-06-26 02:42:50, establishing an algorithmic, sci-fi contrast directly against the retro-fantasy world above it.
