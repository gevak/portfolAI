The website utilizes a top-down, pseudo-3D perspective simulating a sophisticated but thoroughly disorganized workspace. The background is a high-resolution, elegant dark walnut or matte slate desk surface. The UI elements are designed as physical objects scattered across this canvas at varying, organic rotations. To make the "messy desk" interactive and engaging, modern JavaScript and CSS physics are applied: users can drag and drop any object to rearrange the desk, and clicking an item dynamically brings it to the top of the viewport (manipulating the z-index). Hover state CSS applies a smooth, lifting drop-shadow and momentarily straightens the object, inviting the user to read its contents.

The centerpiece of the desk is an open, premium-looking leather-bound notebook or a sleek tablet, sitting slightly askew. Written inside in an elegant, modern sans-serif font is "Geva Kipper", followed by a clean, single-sentence sub-heading detailing his current work at Google. Below this, styled as if printed by a vintage typewriter or a colorful sticky note, is the subtitle: "This workspace is redesigned every day by AI, forever shifting, forever changing."

Section 1 (GlglStats) is designed as a physical vinyl record sleeve partially pulled out, or a piece of scientific graphing paper. The cover art features a colorful LZ77 compression visualization of pop-chart repetitiveness. A glossy, retro-style sticker acts as the link to the https://glglstats.geekip.dev website, while a physical-looking "Play" button or a Polaroid photo of Geva presenting on stage serves as the clickable hyperlink to the PyData TLV conference YouTube video (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo).

Section 2 (Ahuzat Dibuk) takes the form of an overlapping cluster of Israeli parking citations and miniature, readable newspaper clippings. A faint thermal heat-map gradient bleeds into the background of this paper cluster. A realistic red paperclip holds three physical-looking media tabs protruding from the edges: two stylized as news print leading to Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/) and Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and a blue sticky note linking to the original Secret Tel-Aviv Facebook publication (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).

Section 3 (2D Part Orienting) is styled as a cyan architectural blueprint spread out under a heavy, metallic drafting compass. Faint white geometrical wireframes, polygons, and snippets of C++ code are visible on the paper. A transparent, sleek plastic ruler overlaps the blueprint, acting as the clickable hyperlink directing visitors to the computational geometry library page and code at https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/.

Section 4 (The Turing Jest) looks like a vintage comedy club flyer resting beneath a judge's wooden gavel or a boxing referee's scorecard. It features a split-tone design to represent the LMSYS-like competing LLMs, with a tally mark graphic indicating user ratings. A torn admission ticket stub attached to the bottom of the flyer serves as the entrance link to the arena at https://jest.geekip.dev.

Section 5 (Rhymes of Israel) and Section 6 (Meta Portfolio) sit nearby. Section 5 is conceptualized as a torn-out high school yearbook page, complete with doodle-style rhyming graffiti in the margins mimicking comedic news summaries. A glossy, embossed sticker resembling the Chrome logo invites the user to install the extension at https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb. Section 6 embraces a meta approach: it is designed as a small, glass-like Polaroid photo that shows an inception-style, infinite mirror reflection of the very desk the user is looking at. Tapping the Polaroid links back to the root at https://geekip.dev.

Section 7 is represented by a spill of three sleek, minimalist business cards dropped casually near the bottom edge of the desk. When the user hovers over this spill, the cards fan out into a neat, organized row, revealing stylized typography and logos linking to github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper. 

At the bottom right corner of the desk sits the footer: a glowing, modern digital desk clock (resembling an LED or Nixie tube display). Instead of the current time, this clock features a JavaScript-driven stopwatch dynamically ticking continuously, displaying the exact hours, minutes, and seconds that have elapsed since 2026-03-23 01:46:08. Resting against the base of this clock is a small, engraved metallic desk nameplate that reads: "Workspace curated and continuously redesigned by [Insert AI Model Name]".
