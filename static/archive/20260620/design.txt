The global layout stages a hyper-realistic, interactive 3D smartphone bezel floating in the center of the viewport, serving as the website’s entire canvas. The background outside the phone is a dynamic, slowly shifting ambient gradient using webGL or CSS canvas, visually reinforcing the concept of an AI in constant flux. The phone features glassmorphism effects, a subtle glare that moves with mouse tracking, and a physical-looking "home indicator" bar at the bottom. 

The website's landing state is the phone's "Lock Screen." At the top, a sleek digital clock displays the user's local time. Below it, two frosted-glass "push notifications" serve as the introduction. The first notification displays: **Geva Kipper**, alongside the sentence detailing your current work at Google. The second notification acts as the subtitle, displaying: "System Update: This website is re-designed each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." A pulsing "Swipe up to unlock" animation at the bottom invites the user to interact. Upon scrolling or clicking, the lock screen slides up, revealing the "Home Screen."

The Home Screen features a modern, iOS-style grid of 8 app icons, each representing a portfolio section. Clicking an app scales its interface beautifully to fill the entire screen of the virtual phone, utilizing smooth CSS transitions. Users return to the home screen by clicking the "swipe" bar at the bottom. The interior design of each app is tailored to its specific project, turning standard portfolio boxes into immersive micro-experiences:

**App 1: GlglStats (Music Player Aesthetic)**  
The icon resembles an equalizer or a record. Inside, the UI mimics a dark-mode streaming app like Spotify, adorned with neon green accents. A dynamic, animated visualizer plays in the background, symbolizing LZ77 compression and pop-music repetition patterns. The top section presents an embedded YouTube player for the PyData TLV conference, while large, tactile playback buttons serve as the outgoing links to the live project site.

**App 2: Ahuzat Dibuk (Navigation/Map Aesthetic)**  
This app opens to reveal a sleek, dark-themed geographic interface reminiscent of Apple Maps. A glowing red-and-yellow CSS-rendered heatmap overlays a minimalist grid of Tel Aviv. Informational pop-up cards function as the navigation menu, offering cleanly styled buttons to read the news stories on Channel 13 and Mako, alongside a secondary card linking to the original Facebook publication. 

**App 3: 2D Part Orienting (Drafting/CAD Aesthetic)**  
The design shifts to a crisp, bright, grid-paper background, evoking computational geometry and engineering. An animated SVG of a polygon being repeatedly pushed and aligned by a flat plane loops at the top of the app. Below it, clean, monospaced typography details the C++ algorithm and open-source library, followed by a prominent blueprint-style button bridging to the Tau project page and code repository.

**App 4: The Turing Jest (Messaging App Aesthetic)**  
Modeled after a modern chat interface, this app drops the user into an active, fictional conversation between two AI avatars exchanging puns. The chat bubbles provide the project description of the LMSYS-like arena. At the bottom, where a keyboard would normally be, a large "Enter Arena" button links out to the live LLM judging site.

**App 5: Rhymes of Israel (News/Social Media Aesthetic)**  
This app uses a split-screen design. The top half resembles a serious, traditional Israeli news outlet (complete with serif fonts and headline tickers). A visual "glitch" effect periodically strikes the screen, transitioning the bottom half into a playful, doodle-filled highschool yearbook aesthetic. A prominent "Add to Chrome" badge sits at the center, linking directly to the extension store.

**App 6: Hatul Basak (Trivia Game Aesthetic)**  
Opening with an energetic, gamified pop of purple and gold colors, this app represents the Wikipedia-based trivia port. It features an interactive UI mimicking a quiz-show podium. Bright, pill-shaped buttons offer obvious calls-to-action to play daily AI-generated challenges or access the puzzle-creator laboratory, seamlessly integrating the provided link.

**App 7: Portfolio Website (Terminal Aesthetic)**  
Representing the metaproject, this app opens a retro-futuristic, green-on-black command-line interface. "Recursion detected..." types automatically on the screen, followed by a self-referential hyperlink to geekip.dev, creating a fun, developer-focused easter egg experience.

**App 8: Social Links (Contacts App Aesthetic)**  
Designed like a minimalist digital Rolodex or modern address book, this app presents three elegant "Contact Cards." Each card features a high-resolution logo (GitHub, LinkedIn, Facebook) and slides out a detailed view with clickable links to your respective profiles, giving potential recruiters a familiar and satisfying way to connect.

Finally, outside the bounds of the virtual smartphone chassis, resting fixed at the very bottom center of the broader browser window margin, sits an elegant, glowing, frosted-glass footer panel. Within it is a minimalist plaque reading: "Designed and Generated by AI Model," placed adjacent to a live, ticking digital stopwatch written in monospaced neon typography. The stopwatch calculates and actively counts up the exact time in Hours, Minutes, and Seconds elapsed since the anchor timestamp: `2026-06-20 02:44:03`.
