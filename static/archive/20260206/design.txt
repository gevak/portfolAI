# Matrix Rain Portfolio ‚Äî Design Description

## Overall Concept

The entire page opens with a full-screen Matrix-style digital rain animation ‚Äî columns of falling green characters (a mix of katakana, Latin letters, and digits) cascading downward on a pure black background. After approximately three seconds, the falling characters begin to decelerate and drift laterally, clustering together as if magnetically attracted to invisible scaffolding. They assemble ‚Äî character by character, like iron filings snapping to a magnet ‚Äî into the readable text and UI elements of the portfolio. Once assembled, the characters settle into place but retain a subtle, continuous shimmer: individual glyphs randomly flicker to alternate characters for a single frame before returning, so the entire page perpetually looks alive, as though the content could dissolve back into rain at any moment. A very faint, slow rain of dimmed green characters continues falling in the deep background behind all content for the lifetime of the page, ensuring the Matrix atmosphere never fully disappears.

## Color Palette and Typography

The background is a deep true black (#000000). The primary text color is a bright phosphor green (#00FF41), with highlights and hover states in a lighter electric white-green (#7DFDAA). Secondary text and descriptions use a muted green-grey (#4A7A5A). All section borders and divider lines are rendered as a dim green (#1A3A1A) that brightens to full phosphor green on hover. The primary typeface is a monospace font ‚Äî Source Code Pro or Fira Code with ligatures enabled ‚Äî reinforcing the hacker-terminal aesthetic. The developer's name uses a heavier weight of the same monospace family, slightly tracked out. No serif or sans-serif fonts appear anywhere; everything stays in the monospace family to maintain the illusion that the entire site is a living terminal readout.

The subtitle text itself has a very slow character-by-character rewrite animation on a long loop ‚Äî every 20 seconds or so, the text dissolves letter by letter back into random Matrix characters and then reassembles, as if being regenerated.

## Section Cards Layout

Below the header, the six project sections plus the links section (seven total) are arranged in a responsive grid: three columns on desktop, two on tablet, one on mobile. Each section is a rectangular card with a thin single-pixel border in dim green. The interior of each card is not pure black but very slightly lighter (#050505) to create just enough contrast to distinguish the card from the infinite background. Each card has a subtle scanline overlay ‚Äî thin horizontal lines at 2px intervals with very low opacity ‚Äî to give a CRT monitor feel.

When a card enters the viewport during scroll, it doesn't simply fade in ‚Äî instead, a miniature localized rain of green characters falls within the card's bounding box for about one second, and then those characters rearrange and snap into the actual content text of the card, mirroring the opening animation but in miniature and scoped to each card individually.

## Card Interactivity

On hover, each card's border brightens to full phosphor green and gains a soft green box-shadow glow (0 0 15px rgba(0,255,65,0.3)). The card also shifts upward by a few pixels. The background of the hovered card gets an animated rain effect ‚Äî a very faint, slow stream of tiny characters falling behind the text, visible only inside that card. When the cursor leaves, the glow fades and the micro-rain dissipates over half a second.

## Scrolling and Motion

As the user scrolls down, the background rain columns subtly shift in parallax ‚Äî they fall slightly slower than the scroll speed, creating a gentle depth illusion as if the content floats above the rain. The green glow from card borders reflects faintly into the rain immediately surrounding each card, as though the cards are physical monitors illuminating the falling code near them.

A thin, vertical, glowing green scrollbar replaces the browser default ‚Äî styled as a single bright green line on the right edge of the viewport, with a small blinking cursor-block as the thumb.

## Responsive Behavior

On mobile, the grid collapses to a single column. The initial full-screen rain animation is shorter (two seconds) to respect mobile performance. The background rain density is reduced to fewer columns. Card hover effects become tap effects ‚Äî tapping a card triggers the glow and micro-rain briefly. The header name scales down but retains its glow trails. The stopwatch in the footer remains fully functional and legible.

## Performance Considerations

The Matrix rain is rendered on a single HTML5 canvas element positioned fixed behind all content, running at a capped 30fps to conserve resources. Card-level micro-rain animations use CSS animations with transforms and opacity rather than additional canvas layers. The initial assembly animation uses requestAnimationFrame with character position interpolation. All glow effects use CSS box-shadow and text-shadow rather than filter: blur for better performance. The shimmer/flicker effect on settled text is achieved with a lightweight JavaScript interval that randomly toggles characters in a sparse subset of text nodes, affecting only 1-2% of characters at any given frame.
