The website features a sleek, asymmetric split-screen layout enveloped in a dark-mode, cyberpunk-meets-minimalism aesthetic. The background is a deep obsidian void accented by subtle, drifting data particles. On the left side (or sticky at the top on mobile), occupying 40% of the viewport, is the habitat of the Virtual Pet. This pet is rendered as an elegant, 3D holographic geometric creature. It starts at the top of the page as a simple, glowing, pulsing core of light. As the user scrolls down the right side of the page, the core consumes neon data-streams emitting from each project card entering the viewport. With every new project discovered, the pet structurally evolves—growing data-wave limbs for music, a grid-like shell for mapping, sharp geometric facets for C++, expressive digital eyes for AI, and feline ears for trivia—ultimately culminating in a majestic, fully-formed digital companion by the end of the scroll. 

The hero section sits at the top right, featuring the developer's name, **Geva Kipper**, in large, bold typography with a fluid iridescent gradient. Beneath it sits the sentence: "Taking on complex engineering challenges and building scalable software solutions at Google." Directly below, styled in a sleek monospace font with a slow fade-in effect to emphasize its temporal nature, is the subtitle: "This website is redesigned each day by AI and is thus ever-changing." 

The projects are presented as a vertical stack of eight frosted-glass (glassmorphic) cards on the right 60% of the screen. The cards have a semi-transparent dark background, thin neon borders that light up on hover, and smooth parallax effects. 
**Box 1** focuses on 'GlglStats', featuring a hover background resembling a subtle LZ77 audio-compression wave. It contains an elegant call-to-action button linking to https://glglstats.geekip.dev and a secondary video icon linking to the PyData TLV presentation at https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo. 
**Box 2** houses 'Ahuzat Dibuk', utilizing a subtle heatmap color gradient shifting from blue to red. The text details the Tel-Aviv parking scraper, followed by a neat row of external press links labeled for Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/), Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and the original Secret Tel Aviv Facebook post (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/). 
**Box 3** highlights '2D Part Orienting', where hovering reveals a blueprint-style wireframe polygon animation; it includes a link to the open-source CGAL implementation at https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/.

Continuing the scroll, **Box 4** presents 'The Turing Jest' with an icon motif bridging comedy theater masks and neural networks, linking to the LMSYS-like localized arena at https://jest.geekip.dev. 
**Box 5** details the 'Rhymes of Israel' Chrome extension, styled playfully with elements resembling a neon yearbook cutout, directing users to install it via https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb. 
**Box 6** showcases 'Hatul Basak', integrating a faint silhouette of a cat intertwined with Wikipedia puzzle pieces, providing a bold play/create link to https://hatulbasak.geekip.dev. 
**Box 7** creates an "infinity mirror" visual effect within the glass card to represent the meta-nature of the portfolio project itself, pointing via a self-reference link to https://geekip.dev. 
Finally, **Box 8** serves as the connection hub, stripping away heavy text in favor of three minimalist, glowing icons that beat in sync with the fully evolved virtual pet on the left, linking to github.com/gevak, linkedin.com/in/gevakip, and facebook.com/geva.kipper.

At the very bottom, anchored to the viewport once the scroll ends, is a stark, elegant footer. Set in razor-sharp monospace typography, it reads: "Design conceptualized and actively hallucinated by an AI language model." Beside this credit sits a live, ticking digital stopwatch, utilizing a glowing red digital font, precisely calculating the passing hours, minutes, and seconds since the epoch timestamp: 2026-05-04 02:10:59.
