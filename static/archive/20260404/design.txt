**Global Aesthetic and Layout**
The website utilizes a deep, pitch-black background (#050508) to serve as a void for the "glowing nervous system" aesthetic. The primary visual motif is an organic, asymmetrical network of luminous dendrites and axons (implemented via SVG lines and canvas particles) that act as the structural "spine" of the single-page layout. As the user scrolls, "action potentials" (bright pulses of cyan, neon purple, and electric green light) shoot down these neural pathways from the top of the page, triggering the illumination of distinct project nodes. The design relies heavily on modern glassmorphism—translucent, dark-tinted panels with strong CSS box-shadow glows that fade in softly as if a digital organism is waking up. Hovering over any element triggers an "electrical surge" animation along its connecting path.

**The Main Brain (Hero Section)**
At the very top of the hierarchy is the central "Brain" node. The typography for the developer’s name, **Geva Kipper**, is rendered in a sleek, glowing sans-serif font, styled to look like interwoven illuminated fibers. Immediately below, resting inside a subtly pulsating, semi-transparent membrane container, is the bio paragraph: "Currently engineering highly scalable, impactful solutions as a Software Engineer at Google." Below this sits the subtitle, written in a slightly erratic, monospace "terminal" font that occasionally flickers: "Warning: This digital organism is entirely re-designed each day by AI; its form is ever-changing and adapting." Thick, glowing root-nerves extend from the bottom of this section, weaving down the page to connect the subsequent nodes.

**The Synaptic Nodes (Sections 1-6)**
The core content sections are designed as "synaptic terminals"—glassmorphic cards with rounded, organic corners, connected off-center to the main vertical nerve trunk. Each card features an idle faint glow that intensifies dramatically into a specific color upon scroll intersection. 

*   **Node 1: GlglStats (Cyan Glow)** – Designed to evoke rhythmic energy, the internal nerves of this card pulse like audio equalizers. It displays the text for the LZ77 compression and pop-chart repetition project. It features two prominent, glowing call-to-action buttons: one linking to the live experiment (https://glglstats.geekip.dev) and a media-rich embedded thumbnail with a play icon linking to the PyData TLV presentation (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jrKAtOsj1Lo).
*   **Node 2: Ahuzat Dibuk (Amber/Heatmap Glow)** – The background of this card features a subtle, slow-moving heatmap fluid animation, representing the Tel-Aviv parking lot occupancy data. The project description sits above three interconnected media synapses (glowing text links with news or social icons) directing users to Channel 13 (https://13tv.co.il/item/news/domestic/internal/parking-902956407/), Mako (https://www.mako.co.il/nexter-news/Article-ae43964b891bf71027.htm), and the original Facebook publication (https://www.facebook.com/groups/secrettelaviv/posts/10159636301195943/).
*   **Node 3: 2D Part Orienting (Electric Blue Glow)** – This card merges organic nerves with rigid geometric shapes. Slowly rotating glowing polygons sit behind the text, visualizing the C++ computational geometry algorithms. A prominent "Open Source" neural root leads to the project page and code (https://www.cgl.cs.tau.ac.il/projects/2d-part-orienting/).
*   **Node 4: The Turing Jest (Magenta Glow)** – Designed as a dual-lobed synapse, visually representing the combat between two LLMs in the LMSYS-like arena. The text explains the pun-making combat, leading to an interactive-looking terminal portal link (https://jest.geekip.dev). 
*   **Node 5: Rhymes of Israel (Neon Yellow Glow)** – A playful node where a stylized Chrome logo merges organically into a neural network. The text describes the highschool yearbook-style LLM headline rewriter, connected to an "Install Extension" glowing node leading to the Chrome Web Store (https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/eainginjgdkpgolpjniijdihfbeeeklb).
*   **Node 6: The Meta-Portfolio (Iridescent Glow)** – A recursively designed card that holds a miniature, glowing version of the entire website's neural map within it. The text acknowledges the meta-nature of the AI-designed site, featuring a self-referential feedback-loop link back to itself (https://geekip.dev).

**The Peripheral System (Section 7) & Temporal Footer**
The main nerve trunk splits at the bottom into three distinct, fine dendrites. Each connects to a circular, minimalist glowing icon representing the developer's digital footprint: GitHub (github.com/gevak), LinkedIn (linkedin.com/in/gevakip), and Facebook (facebook.com/geva.kipper). Hovering over one sends a rapid electrical spark back up the main trunk.

Finally, the roots fade into the dark abyss of the footer. Centered at the bottom in a muted, ghostly white font is the AI attribution text: "Neural pathways synthesized and designed by an AI model." directly beneath this is the Temporal Counter—a digital stopwatch styled like a biological heartbeat monitor, dynamically ticking to display the exact time (Hours:Minutes:Seconds) elapsed since the baseline epoch: "2026-04-04 01:31:00".
