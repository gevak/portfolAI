GlglStats
SECTOR 01

A comprehensive analytics platform for Israeli music streaming data. GlglStats scrapes, processes, and visualizes chart performance across major streaming platforms using Python, enabling deep insights into listening patterns, genre trends, and artist trajectories. The project was presented at PyData TLV, demonstrating the data pipeline architecture and visualization techniques employed to surface meaningful patterns from raw chart data.

Ahuzat Dibuk
SECTOR 02

An investigative data project that scraped and mapped parking enforcement data across Tel Aviv, revealing hidden patterns in municipal ticketing activity. Through data visualization, heat-maps, and statistical analysis, the project exposed systematic biases and disproportionate enforcement in certain neighborhoods. The findings gained significant public attention, covered by major Israeli news outlets including Channel 13 and Mako, sparking civic discourse about transparent urban governance.

2D Part Orienting
SECTOR 03

A computational geometry research project developed at Tel Aviv University, tackling the problem of orienting arbitrary 2D polygonal parts using a sequence of push operations. Implemented in C++, the algorithm computes minimal push-plans that can deterministically orient a given polygon from any initial configuration. The work addresses fundamental challenges in computational geometry and automated manufacturing, where parts on a conveyor must be oriented before assembly without visual feedback.

The Turing Jest
SECTOR 04

An interactive arena where Large Language Models compete head-to-head in the ancient art of punning. Users are presented with puns generated by different LLMs and vote on which is funnier, building an ELO-style rating system across models. The Turing Jest explores the frontier of AI humor, testing whether machines can master the most groan-worthy form of human wit. The platform surfaces surprising differences in comedic style across model architectures.

Rhymes of Israel
SECTOR 05

A Chrome extension that transforms the mundane experience of reading news headlines into poetic delight. Powered by LLM technology, Rhymes of Israel rewrites Hebrew news headlines so they rhyme, injecting levity into the daily scroll through current events. The extension intercepts page content in real-time, sends headlines through an AI rewriting pipeline, and seamlessly replaces the original text — turning every news feed into an inadvertent poetry reading.
