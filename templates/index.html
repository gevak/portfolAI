Log Entry: The Isle of Echoes (GlglStats)

A Python project analyzing the music played on Galgalatz, Israel's most popular radio station. It scrapes years of playlists, processes the data, and presents insights via a public dashboard. This cartographer's findings were even presented at a PyData conference.

Log Entry: The Gridlock Archipelago (Ahuzat Dibuk)

A data-driven investigation into the scarcity of residents' parking in Tel Aviv. By cross-referencing official data on parking permits and available spots, this project revealed a significant city-wide deficit, sparking conversation and media coverage in major Israeli news outlets.

Log Entry: The Crystalline Atoll (2D Part Orienting)

A C++ project in computational geometry from Tel-Aviv University. The goal was to find the optimal orientation (by angle) for a 2D polygonal "part" to minimize its bounding box area, a common problem in manufacturing and packing. The solution involves complex geometric algorithms and data structures.

Log Entry: The Jester's Rock (The Turing Jest)

Can you tell which comedian is an AI? 'The Turing Jest' is a web-based arena where users are presented with two jokes—one by a human comedian, one by an LLM—and must vote for the funnier one. It's a playful exploration of AI creativity and humor, complete with leaderboards and stats.

Log Entry: The Scribe's Peninsula (Rhymes of Israel)

A simple but useful Chrome extension for Hebrew poets and songwriters. This tool adds an icon to the browser that, when clicked, opens a pop-up to a Hebrew rhyming dictionary website, providing quick and easy access to creative resources without interrupting the writing workflow.
